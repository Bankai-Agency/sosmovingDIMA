Tips on Moving in the Off-Peak Season

Tips on Moving in the Off-Peak Season

Last Updated: 
Thursday, March 4, 2021

Table of Contents

    There are lots of things that go into planning a relocation well. Not only do you have tasks like hiring movers Los Angeles, packing boxes, and finding your dream home, but you also need to pick the right time for moving. When it comes to relocating, there are certain months that are considered to be the peak of the moving season. That’s when most people tend to book their moving dates because it’s the most convenient time for relocating. However, if you’re moving in the off-peak season, you’re not making a mistake. On the contrary, there are quite a few perks that come with not moving at the peak of the season. To learn a bit more about those perks and get tips for your off-season move, keep reading.

    When is the peak of the moving season?

    As we’ve said, there are a few months that are known to be the peak of the moving season. To specify, those are the summer months, so from June to August. This is when most people have time off work, kids are out of school and that makes for a convenient time for moving. Therefore most people schedule their moving days for the summer.

    And while moving in the summer is great because you have free time, it’s not the cheapest. Because the movers are very busy during these months and the demand is high, moving companies’ prices go up. This is the most expensive period for moving, so that’s a big reason while some people prefer moving in the off-peak season. But that’s not the only thing that makes avoiding the peak of the season a good idea – let’s see some other perks.

    The perks of moving in the off-peak season

    It’s cheaper!

    As we’ve mentioned, the prices of moving services go up during the summer. So, if you’re moving sometime between September and May, you can expect to pay less than you would in the summer. Just ask your movers for moving quotes LA and compare the prices. You’ll see that this is a great way to save some money and reduce moving expenses if you’re flexible enough to wait for these months. That’s the first thing that makes off-season relocating great.

    Colder weather makes relocating in the off-peak season easier than relocating in the peak of the season

    The heat, sweat, and stuffy air can make moving quite difficult. You tend to feel a bit sluggish and tired when it’s really hot outside and having to carry boxes and move your household is the last thing you feel like doing. That’s why moving in the colder months is great.

    Carrying the boxes and moving into your new home is much easier when you’re not dealing with the summer heat.

    It’s easier to breathe and be active when the sun isn’t as harsh. So, not only will you save some coins, but it will also be easier for you and your movers to carry out all the moving tasks when the weather is milder. Of course, if you’re moving in the cold, cold winter, you could face some other issues, but more on that later.

    There are more available dates and moving companies

    Because the demand isn’t so high as there are fewer people moving in the off-season, the movers are more flexible. There are move moving companies available and they offer more moving dates. This means you’ll be able to choose a day that suits you best and count on the movers being available. So, you don’t have to worry if you’ll be able to get packing services LA and any other professional assistance you need. You can trust that you can get whichever services you need.

    Tips for an off-peak season move

    • Double wrap your items. Wrapping your things is always a good choice, but when it’s snowing or raining, it’s even more important. To provide extra protection for your things when moving in the winter, add an extra layer of bubble pack or whatever you’re using for wrapping.
    • Dress appropriately when moving in the off-peak season. Wear something warm and comfortable – it’s best to go for layers. Also, choose your shoes wisely – you don’t want to get wet feet!

    If you’re moving in the winter, there are some things you should keep in mind and be wary of to avoid any issues.

    • Drive slowly and carefully. Icy roads, snow, and fog can make for a dangerous situation when driving, so be careful.
    • Get winter equipment for your vehicle.

    Fall and spring have the ideal weather conditions for moving

    If you’re relocating in the off-peak season, you can choose the season and the month that works best for you. When it comes to weather conditions, fall and spring are usually the ideal choices. The weather is mild and perfect for any kind of outdoor activity, including moving. It’s not too cold or too hot, plus the prices of residential movers LA are lower.

    But there is one thing you should keep in mind – your kids’ school. If the move requires them to start a new school, that could be tricky once the school year has already started. So, pay attention to that if you’re moving in the fall or spring.

    Pick the right day for the move

    Not only is it important to pick the right month for moving in the off-peak season, but choosing the right day is of importance too. Namely, there are certain parts of the month that are better for moving than others. First of all, it’s best to move in the middle of the month when there aren’t as many people who are relocating. The first and the last few days of the month are usually the busiest for moving companies – that’s when leases end. So, avoid the high traffic days and move in the middle of the month to save some extra money and have more flexibility. For the same reasons, it’s best to move on a weekday instead of the weekend. Combine all these tips and you’ll get a great deal for the moving services and you’ll be able to choose the date and time that suits you best.
