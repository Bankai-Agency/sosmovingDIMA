How to Have an Eco-Friendly Move: 7 Sustainable Tips

How to Have an Eco-Friendly Move: 7 Sustainable Tips

Last Updated: 
Monday, September 8, 2025

Table of Contents

    Moving is a process that can generate a surprising amount of waste, from cardboard boxes and packing tape to the carbon footprint of the moving truck. The good news is that with a little planning, you can make your relocation significantly more sustainable. At SOS Moving, we believe in responsible moving. Here are our top 7 tips for an eco-friendly move that’s kind to the planet.

    1. Declutter and Donate Before You Pack

    The most sustainable thing you can move is nothing at all. Before you pack a single box, go through your belongings and decide what you truly need. Moving fewer items means using a smaller truck (or less space), which reduces fuel consumption.

    • Donate: Give gently used clothing, furniture, and household goods to local charities.
    • Sell: Use online marketplaces to sell items that are still in good condition.
    • Recycle: Properly dispose of old electronics and other recyclable materials.

    2. Choose Eco-Friendly Packing Supplies

    Think beyond brand-new cardboard boxes and plastic bubble wrap.

    • Rent Reusable Bins: Many companies offer plastic moving bin rentals. They are sturdy, stackable, and are reused hundreds of times, eliminating cardboard waste.
    • Find Used Boxes: Check local community groups, online marketplaces, or grocery and liquor stores for free used boxes that are still in good shape.
    • Use What You Already Own: Pack items in suitcases, laundry baskets, and storage bins you already have. Use your towels, linens, and clothing to wrap fragile items instead of packing paper.
    • Eco-Friendly Bubble Wrap Alternatives: Look for biodegradable packing peanuts or recycled packing paper.

    3. Recycle and Dispose of Materials Responsibly

    After you’ve unpacked, make a plan for your moving materials. Break down all cardboard boxes and ensure they go into the recycling bin. If you have packing peanuts, check if a local shipping store will accept them for reuse.

    4. Hire a "Green" Moving Company

    When choosing a moving company, ask about their sustainability practices. A responsible mover like SOS Moving focuses on efficiency, which is inherently eco-friendly. We plan the most direct routes to save fuel and aim to consolidate shipments when possible for long-distance moves. A professional company is also less likely to damage items, which reduces waste from having to replace broken belongings.

    5. Use Non-Toxic Cleaning Supplies

    When cleaning out your old home and preparing your new one, opt for non-toxic, biodegradable cleaning products. You can easily make your own effective cleaners using simple ingredients like vinegar and baking soda.

    6. Pack a "Zero-Waste" Essentials Box

    For moving day itself, pack your essentials box with sustainability in mind. Instead of disposable plates and water bottles, include a set of reusable plates, cutlery, and refillable water bottles for your family.

    7. Plan Your Trip Efficiently

    If you are driving to your new home on a long-distance move, use a mapping app to find the most direct and fuel-efficient route. Combining your move with any other necessary errands can also help reduce your carbon footprint.

    Conclusion

    An eco-friendly move doesn't have to be complicated. By making a few conscious choices to reduce, reuse, and recycle, you can significantly lessen the environmental impact of your relocation. Moving to a new home is a fresh start—why not make it a green one?
