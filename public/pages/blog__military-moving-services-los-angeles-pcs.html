Last Updated:

Table of Contents

    Military families face unique moving challenges that civilian movers rarely understand. PCS orders arrive with minimal notice, weight allowances affect reimbursement, temporary storage becomes essential between assignments, and families often manage moves while service members deploy. Los Angeles hosts major military installations including Los Angeles Air Force Base, Fort MacArthur, and Naval Base Ventura County, making military relocations common throughout the region. SOS Moving provides military-focused moving services, understanding the specific requirements, timelines, and stresses military families navigate.

    Understanding Military Moves

    PCS (Permanent Change of Station) basics:Orders typically provide 2-6 months notice. Government covers moving expenses within limits. Weight allowances based on rank and dependents. Personally Procured Move (PPM) option available. Temporary storage authorized often.

    Types of military moves:

    • HHG (Household Goods): Government contracts moving company
    • PPM/DITY: Do-It-Yourself with reimbursement
    • Partial PPM: Combination of both
    • TDY/TCS: Temporary moves under 6 months
    • Retirement/Separation: Final move benefits

    LA military installations:

    • Los Angeles Air Force Base (El Segundo)
    • Naval Base Ventura County (Point Mugu/Port Hueneme)
    • Fort MacArthur (San Pedro)
    • Multiple Reserve/Guard centers
    • Coast Guard facilities

    PPM/DITY Move Advantages

    Financial benefits:Government pays 95% of what they'd pay contractors. Families often profit doing moves themselves. Tax-free payment if under allowance. Control over timeline and handling.

    SOS Moving PPM support:

    • Certified weight tickets provided
    • Military discount rates
    • Flexible scheduling for orders
    • Documentation for reimbursement
    • Storage solutions included

    Weight allowance by rank:

    • E-1 to E-4: 7,000-8,000 lbs
    • E-5: 9,000 lbs
    • E-6: 11,000 lbs
    • E-7 to E-9: 12,000-13,000 lbs
    • O-1 to O-2: 10,000-12,500 lbs
    • O-3 to O-4: 13,500-16,500 lbs
    • O-5+: 17,500-18,000 lbs

    Common Military Moving Scenarios

    Deployment preparation:Service member deploying mid-move. Spouse handling everything alone. Power of attorney requirements. Storage during deployment. Car shipping coordination.

    Overseas to CONUS:Household goods arriving separately. Temporary lodging coordination. Vehicle shipping delays. Unaccompanied baggage management. Cultural adjustment support.

    Retirement/Separation moves:Final move entitlements. Home of record options. Weight allowance considerations. Timeline flexibility increased. Transition assistance available.

    Emergency moves:Humanitarian orders immediate. Family emergency reassignments. Medical evacuation situations. Expedited processing required. Stress levels extreme.

    • Government storage: Free but limited access

    • SOS Moving: Military-friendly terms
    • Climate-controlled: Electronics/valuables
    • Vehicle storage: Deployment special rates

    Storage costs:Government authorized: Covered fully. Self-procured: Reimbursable to limit. Our military rate: $150-300/month. Access fees: Often waived. Insurance: Included typically.

    LA-Specific Military Challenges

    Base housing variations:Air Force Base housing modern but limited. Navy housing waiting lists extensive. Off-base BAH varies significantly. School district considerations important. Commute planning essential.

    Popular military neighborhoods:El Segundo: Near Air Force Base. San Pedro: Fort MacArthur proximity. Torrance: Central to bases. Redondo Beach: Beach community popular. Ventura County: Navy families concentrated.

    Traffic considerations:LAX proximity affects timing. 405/105 interchange nightmarish. Beach communities summer congested. Base access hours restricted. Security checks add time.

    Supporting Military Spouses

    Unique spouse challenges:Managing moves solo often. Employment disruption repeated. School transitions for children. Social network rebuilding. Career license transfers.

    How we help:Flexible scheduling around duties. Single point of contact. Clear communication always. Extra support provided. Understanding and patience.

    Documentation Requirements

    Essential paperwork:Orders (PCS/TDY/Separation). Weight tickets certified. Receipts for everything. Inventory detailed completely. Insurance documentation.

    For reimbursement:DD Form 2278 (Application). Weight tickets (empty/full). Receipts organized clearly. Mileage documentation. Operating expense records.

    SOS Moving provides:Official weight certificates. Detailed inventory lists. Insurance documentation. Receipt organization assistance. Military-familiar paperwork.

    Special Considerations

    Security clearance items:Classified materials excluded. Government equipment separate. Personal weapons declared. Sensitive documents secured. Background checks understood.

    Military equipment:Uniforms require special care. Awards/decorations protected carefully. TA-50 gear separately tracked. Personal armor authorized sometimes. Government property identified.

    Family considerations:Exceptional Family Member Program needs. School records transferred. Medical records organized. Pet transportation arranged. Vehicle registration planned.

    Timeline Management

    Typical PCS timeline:

    • Orders received: Start planning immediately
    • 60 days out: Schedule movers
    • 30 days out: Begin packing/purging
    • 14 days out: Confirm arrangements
    • Moving day: Execute plan
    • Arrival: File claims if needed

    Compressed timelines:Short-notice orders common. Two-week moves possible. Emergency assistance available. Priority scheduling offered. Stress management crucial.
