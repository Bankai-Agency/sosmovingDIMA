Movers Near Me - How to Choose the Right Company

Last Updated: 
Friday, November 21, 2025

Table of Contents

    Typing "movers near me" into Google returns hundreds of Los Angeles moving companies, from legitimate professionals to guys with a truck listed on Craigslist. The difference between choosing right and wrong can mean damaged furniture, surprise fees, or worse—held hostage belongings. After years in the LA moving industry, here's exactly how to separate professional movers from potential disasters.

    Red Flags to Run From

    No physical address: Only PO Box or residential address? They'll disappear with your deposit.

    Too-good-to-be-true quotes: $50/hour for 4 movers? That's below minimum wage. Expect surprise charges.

    Cash only: Legitimate companies like SOS Moving accept cards. Cash-only means no accountability.

    No license number: California requires CPUC licensing. No number = illegal operation.

    Borrowed photos: Reverse image search their trucks. Stock photos = fake company.

    New website: Check domain age. Scammers create sites, scam, disappear, repeat.

    Aggressive sales: "Book today or price doubles!" Professional movers don't use high-pressure tactics.

    Essential Questions to Ask

    "What's your CPUC license number?"Every legal California mover has one. SOS Moving and legitimate companies provide it immediately.

    "Can I visit your office?"Real companies have real locations. Excuses mean they're operating from someone's garage.

    "What's included in your hourly rate?"SOS Moving includes truck, fuel, equipment, insurance. Others charge separately for everything.

    "Do you charge double drive time?"California law requires it for 10+ miles. If they say no, they're lying or will surprise you later.

    "What's your damage policy?"Should be clear, in writing. "We're careful" isn't a policy.

    "Can you provide recent references?"Last month's customers, not reviews from 2019.

    Understanding Moving Quotes

    Legitimate quote includes:

    • Number of movers
    • Hourly rate
    • Minimum hours
    • Truck size
    • Travel time policy
    • Additional fees clearly listed
    • Insurance coverage
    • Payment methods

    SOS Moving quote example:

    • 3 movers: $159/hour (cash) or $169/hour (card)
    • 3-hour minimum
    • Includes truck, fuel, equipment
    • Wardrobe boxes free (returnable)
    • Shrink wrap and tape included
    • Extra charges only for special items (piano: $200)

    Suspicious quote signs:

    • Vague language
    • "Approximately" everywhere
    • No mention of minimums
    • Missing insurance information
    • Cubic feet estimates (for local moves)

    Verify Before You Trust

    Check CPUC license:Go to CPUC website. Enter company name. Verify active status, insurance, complaints.

    Google the phone number:Same number associated with multiple company names? Scam operation using different identities.

    Read recent reviews:Look for patterns. All 5-stars posted same week? Fake. Mix of 4-5 stars over time? Real.

    Check Better Business Bureau:Not foolproof but shows complaint patterns.

    Ask for insurance certificate:Legitimate movers email it immediately. Excuses = red flag.

    Price Shopping Reality Check

    Typical LA moving rates:

    • 2 movers: $100-150/hour
    • 3 movers: $150-200/hour
    • 4 movers: $200-250/hour

    Significantly below = cutting corners somewhere. Significantly above = overcharging unless specialty service.

    Hidden fees to ask about:

    • Fuel surcharge (SOS: $30)
    • Stair fees (SOS: included)
    • Long carry charges (SOS: included)
    • Packing materials
    • Credit card processing
    • Weekend premiums
    • Deposit requirements

    Deposit Warning Signs

    Legitimate deposit policy:

    • Small amount ($50-200)
    • Applies to final bill
    • Credit card accepted
    • Refundable with notice

    Scam deposit signs:

    • Large deposit (50%+)
    • Cash only
    • Wire transfer requested
    • Non-refundable
    • Pressure to pay immediately

    SOS Moving requires just $50 deposit applied to your final bill.

    Day-of-Move Red Flags

    Crew arrives and:

    • Different company name on truck
    • Demands cash before starting
    • "Forgot" equipment, needs more money
    • Says quote was "per mover per hour"
    • Wants to renegotiate price
    • No uniforms or identification

    Professional movers arrive prepared, uniformed, and honor original quotes.

    Online Review Investigation

    Real reviews include:

    • Specific details
    • Both positives and negatives
    • Response from company
    • Reviewer has other reviews
    • Photos of actual move

    Fake reviews obvious by:

    • Generic praise
    • No specifics
    • All posted within days
    • Reviewers have no other activity
    • Similar writing style

    The Craigslist Gamble

    Sure, "Two guys with truck $40/hour" seems appealing. But consider:

    • No insurance
    • No workers comp
    • No accountability
    • No equipment
    • No experience
    • Your friend's couch becomes your problem

    Making the Safe Choice

    Professional movers provide:

    • Written estimates
    • Clear contracts
    • Insurance protection
    • Trained crews
    • Proper equipment
    • Physical location
    • License numbers
    • References

    Worth paying for:

    • Peace of mind
    • Damage protection
    • Professional service
    • Legal recourse
    • No surprises

    Before booking any "movers near me":

    • ✓ Verified CPUC license
    • ✓ Confirmed insurance
    • ✓ Got written estimate
    • ✓ Understood all fees
    • ✓ Checked recent reviews
