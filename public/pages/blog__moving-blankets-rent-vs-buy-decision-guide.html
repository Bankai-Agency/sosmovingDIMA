Moving Blankets - Rent vs Buy Decision Guide

Moving Blankets - Rent vs Buy Decision Guide

Last Updated: 
Saturday, January 24, 2026

Table of Contents

    Moving blankets protect your furniture like bubble wrap protects your grandmother's china—essential, but the cost adds up fast. Buying quality moving blankets costs $120-200 for a dozen, while renting runs $36 daily for the same set. After one move, buying seems obvious. After calculating storage, depreciation, and actual usage patterns, the math gets complicated. Here's the complete analysis for choosing between renting and buying moving blankets in LA.

    What Moving Blankets Actually Do

    Protection provided:

    • Scratch prevention
    • Dent resistance
    • Corner padding
    • Surface protection
    • Dust coverage
    • Minor impact absorption

    What they don't do:

    • Prevent major damage
    • Replace careful handling
    • Protect from water
    • Stop crushing forces
    • Eliminate need for boxes

    Essential for furniture, useless for boxes

    Rental Cost Reality

    Typical rental rates:

    • Basic blankets: $2-4/day each
    • Professional grade: $3-5/day each
    • Minimum rental: Usually 12 blankets
    • Daily cost: $36-60 for set

    Rental duration breakdown:

    • Local move: 1-2 days
    • Long distance: 7-14 days
    • DIY move: 3-5 days
    • Storage transition: 7-30 days

    Cost examples:

    • Weekend move: $72-120
    • Week-long move: $252-420
    • Month storage: $1,080-1,800

    Purchase Cost Analysis

    Quality tiers available:

    Budget blankets ($5-8 each):

    • Thin padding
    • Tears easily
    • Poor stitching
    • Single use quality
    • Harbor Freight special

    Mid-grade ($8-12 each):

    • Decent thickness
    • Adequate protection
    • Multiple use capable
    • Most common choice

    Professional grade ($15-25 each):

    • Heavy duty construction
    • Reinforced edges
    • Washable
    • Long-term durability

    Quantity needed: 12-24 blankets typical

    Usage Pattern Analysis

    One-time movers:

    • Single move only
    • Rental makes sense
    • No storage needed
    • Fresh quality guaranteed

    Frequent movers:

    • Military families
    • Corporate relocations
    • Renters (annual moves)
    • Purchase justified

    Occasional movers:

    • Every 3-5 years
    • Purchase borderline
    • Storage consideration critical

    Helpers/family:

    • Multiple people borrowing
    • Purchase valuable
    • Shared cost possible

    Breakeven Calculation

    Professional rental vs purchase:

    • Rental: $3/day each
    • Purchase: $15 each (professional)
    • Breakeven: 5 days usage

    Budget rental vs purchase:

    • Rental: $3/day each
    • Purchase: $8 each (mid-grade)
    • Breakeven: 2.7 days usage

    Seems like purchase always wins, but..

    ‍

    Hidden Purchase Costs

    Additional expenses:

    • Storage space value: $20-50/month
    • Cleaning supplies: $20
    • Mice damage: $50-100
    • Replacement (wear): $40/year
    • Moving blankets to new home: $50

    Annual ownership cost: $300-700

    Makes rental competitive

    Storage Realities

    Storage requirements:

    • 12 blankets = 4 cubic feet
    • Clean, dry location needed
    • Pest protection required
    • Easy access preferred

    LA storage challenges:

    • Apartments lack space
    • Garages hot/humid
    • Mice love fabric
    • Mildew possible

    Storage costs:

    • Self-storage: $50-150/month
    • Garage space: Valuable
    • Closet space: Limited

    Storage negates purchase savings

    Quality Comparison

    Rental blanket quality:

    • Professional grade usually
    • Well-maintained
    • Regularly cleaned
    • Consistent thickness

    Purchase quality varies:

    • You choose grade
    • Condition degrades
    • Cleaning your responsibility
    • Inconsistent replacement

    Rental often higher quality than budget purchase

    Convenience Factor

    Rental advantages:

    • No storage needed
    • Fresh quality
    • Professional grade
    • No maintenance
    • Return when done

    Purchase advantages:

    • Always available
    • No rental scheduling
    • Familiar equipment
    • Multiple uses possible

    Convenience has value

    LA-Specific Considerations

    Rental availability:

    • U-Haul locations everywhere
    • Moving companies rent
    • Equipment rental stores
    • Good availability

    Purchase options:

    • Harbor Freight (budget)
    • Home Depot (mid-grade)
    • Moving supply stores (professional)
    • Online ordering

    Storage challenges:LA housing lacks storage space universally

    Professional Recommendations

    Buy if you:

    • Move every 2-3 years
    • Have adequate storage
    • Help others move
    • Own valuable furniture
    • DIY frequently

    Rent if you:

    • Move once per decade
    • Lack storage space
    • Want professional quality
    • Need many blankets
    • Prefer convenience

    Most people should rent

    Size and Quantity Guidelines

    Standard sizes:

    • 72" × 80" (most common)
    • 54" × 72" (smaller items)
    • 90" × 90" (large furniture)

    Quantity guidelines:

    • Studio: 6-8 blankets
    • 1-bedroom: 8-12 blankets
    • 2-bedroom: 12-16 blankets
    • 3-bedroom: 16-24 blankets

    Buy exact quantities needed

    Environmental Impact

    Purchase impact:

    • Manufacturing resources
    • Shipping emissions
    • Storage requirements
    • Eventually disposal

    Rental efficiency:

    • Shared usage
    • Professional maintenance
    • Longer lifespan
    • Better utilization

    Rental more sustainable

    Make Your Decision

    Decision framework:

    1. Calculate annual moving frequency
    2. Assess storage availability
    3. Determine quality needs
    4. Consider convenience value
    5. Calculate total cost of ownership

    For most LA residents: Rent

    • Storage limitations
    • Infrequent usage
    • Professional quality
    • No maintenance
