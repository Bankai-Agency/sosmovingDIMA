California to Texas Moving: Costs, Timeline & Tips

Last Updated: 
Saturday, September 27, 2025

Table of Contents

    Thousands of Californians move to Texas monthly, with Los Angeles to Austin being the most popular route. As experienced long-distance movers, SOS Moving has perfected this 1,400-mile journey. Here's what you need to know.

    Real Moving Costs: California to Texas

    Studio/1-Bedroom: $3,000-4,500
    2-Bedroom: $4,500-6,500
    3-Bedroom: $6,500-9,000
    4+ Bedroom: $9,000-15,000

    Additional Costs:

    • Vehicle shipping: $800-1,400
    • Packing materials: $300-800
    • Travel expenses: $500-1,500
    • Texas registration/licenses: $200-400

    Popular Routes & Timeline

    Los Angeles to Austin: 1,379 miles (2-5 days delivery)
    LA to Houston: 1,556 miles (2-5 days delivery)
    San Diego to Dallas: 1,360 miles (2-5 days delivery)

    Moving truck transit typically takes 2-5 business days. Add 1-2 days for pickup and delivery windows.

    30-Day Moving Timeline

    30 Days Before:

    • Book your interstate moving company
    • Start decluttering (less weight = lower cost)
    • Research Texas neighborhoods
    • Begin packing non-essentials

    14 Days Before:

    • Confirm moving dates with SOS Moving
    • Change address with USPS
    • Transfer utilities
    • Pack everything except essentials

    Moving Week:

    • Final walkthrough
    • Check inventory list
    • Keep important documents with you
    • Get cash for tips and travel

    Texas Registration Requirements

    You have:

    • 90 days to get Texas driver's license ($33)
    • 30 days to register vehicle (~$75 plus taxes)
    • Vehicle inspection required ($25-40)

    • Storage options in both states

    Top Texas Cities for Californians

    Austin: Tech hub, liberal, expensive for Texas
    Houston: Diverse, affordable, humid
    Dallas: Corporate jobs, suburbs, real seasons
    San Antonio: Military presence, lowest costs

    Money-Saving Tips

    1. Move October-March (20-30% cheaper than summer)
    2. Declutter aggressively (every pound costs money)
    3. Pack yourself (save $1,000-2,000)
    4. Be flexible with dates (mid-week cheaper)
    5. Compare quotes (prices vary significantly)

    What Makes This Move Unique

    The Good:

    • No state income tax (save 5-13%)
    • Housing 40% cheaper
    • Growing job market
    • Business-friendly environment

    The Adjustments:

    • Humidity and heat more intense
    • Everything's spread out (car essential)
    • Different political climate
    • Tornado season is real

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    • Not checking mover's interstate license
    • Underestimating Texas summer heat
    • Forgetting to budget for AC costs
    • Not researching specific neighborhoods
    • Assuming Texas is all the same
