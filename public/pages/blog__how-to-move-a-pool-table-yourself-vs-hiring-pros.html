How to Move a Pool Table Yourself vs Hiring Pros

How to Move a Pool Table Yourself vs Hiring Pros

Last Updated: 
Friday, January 16, 2026

Table of Contents

    That 8-foot pool table looks manageable until you learn the slate alone weighs 800 pounds and costs $3,000 to replace when it cracks. Every year, LA emergency rooms treat dozens of pool table moving injuries, from herniated discs to crushed feet. The DIY vs professional debate ends quickly when you understand physics, liability, and the very real possibility of destroying your $5,000 table. Here's why moving a pool table yourself ranks among the worst DIY decisions possible.

    Pool Table Anatomy Lesson

    What you're actually moving:

    • Slate bed: 700-1,000 pounds
    • Frame: 200-300 pounds
    • Rails and pockets: 50 pounds
    • Felt: Delicate, tears easily
    • Total weight: 1,000-1,300 pounds

    Slate specifications:

    • 3/4" to 1" thick
    • Usually 3 pieces
    • Precisely leveled
    • Cracks if flexed
    • Cannot be repaired

    One crack = table destroyed

    The DIY Disaster Scenario

    What DIY guides suggest:"Get 4-6 strong friends, rent dollies, be careful"

    What actually happens:

    • Friends bail last minute
    • Slate cracks from flexing
    • Someone gets injured
    • Doorways too narrow
    • Stairs become impossible
    • Felt tears
    • Table never level again

    Success rate: Under 20%Injury rate: Over 50%Damage rate: 75%

    Professional Method

    How pros actually do it:

    1. Photograph current setup
    2. Remove pockets and rails
    3. Remove felt carefully
    4. Separate slate pieces
    5. Transport individually
    6. Reassemble precisely
    7. Re-felt professionally
    8. Level perfectly

    Time: 4-6 hoursCrew: 3-4 specialistsEquipment: $2,000 worth

    Cost Comparison Reality

    DIY true costs:

    • Truck rental: $150
    • Equipment rental: $100
    • Friends' bribes: $200
    • Your time (2 days): $500
    • Felt replacement: $300
    • Medical bills: $500+
    • Slate replacement: $3,000
    • New table (when destroyed): $5,000
    • Potential total: $9,750

    Professional costs:

    • Standard move: $300-500
    • With stairs: $400-600
    • Long distance: $500-800
    • Complete service: Guaranteed
    • Total: $300-800

    Professionals 95% cheaper considering risk

    Equipment Requirements

    DIY equipment needed:

    • Pool table dolly (specialized)
    • Slate lifters
    • Socket wrenches
    • Staple gun
    • Level
    • Moving blankets
    • Proper truck
    • Lifting straps

    Cost to buy: $800Cost to rent: $200Chance of having: 0%

    Pros own everything already

    Physical Requirements

    Lifting reality:

    • Slate piece: 250-350 pounds
    • Requires 4 people minimum
    • Perfect coordination essential
    • One person slips = disaster

    Injury types common:

    • Back injuries (permanent)
    • Hernias
    • Crushed feet/hands
    • Shoulder damage
    • Knee injuries

    Your health worth $300 savings?

    The Slate Problem

    Why slate is impossible:

    • Zero flexibility tolerance
    • Hairline cracks spread
    • Must stay perfectly flat
    • Cannot twist even slightly
    • Temperature sensitive

    DIY slate handling:"Careful" isn't enough. Physics wins.

    Professional handling:Individual padding, specialized carriers, perfect support

    Doorway Navigation

    Standard doorway: 32" widePool table width: 44-54"

    DIY solution:Remove doors, hope, pray

    Professional solution:Disassembly, easy passage, reassembly

    Stuck in doorway stories:Countless. Expensive. Embarrassing.

    Staircase Impossibility

    Pool table + stairs = nightmare

    DIY attempts:

    • Sliding down (crashes)
    • Carrying (injuries)
    • Crane through window ($2,000)

    Professional method:

    • Complete disassembly
    • Piece-by-piece transport
    • Safe, systematic

    Never attempt stairs yourself

    Re-Assembly Complexity

    After moving (if successful):

    • Slate alignment critical
    • Leveling required (precise)
    • Felt installation ($300)
    • Pocket alignment
    • Bumper adjustment

    DIY result:Table never plays right again

    Professional result:Perfect playing surface

    Insurance Considerations

    DIY damage:

    • Homeowners might not cover
    • "Negligence" exclusion
    • No recourse

    Professional damage:

    • Covered by their insurance
    • Liability protection
    • Claims process exists

    One crack = $3,000 claim

    Time Investment

    DIY timeline:

    • Day 1: Disassembly attempt (8 hours)
    • Day 2: Transport and reassembly (10 hours)
    • Day 3: Trying to level (gives up)
    • Week 2: Calling professionals to fix

    Professional timeline:

    • Single day
    • 4-6 hours
    • Complete service

    The Felt Factor

    Removing felt:

    • Must be perfect
    • Usually tears
    • Cannot reuse often

    Installing felt:

    • Professional skill required
    • Special tools needed
    • $300-500 if damaged

    DIY felt installation: Disaster guaranteed

    Common DIY Mistakes

    "We'll just slide it"Frame warps, slate cracks

    "We don't need to disassemble"Doorway damage, injuries

    "The slate won't break"Famous last words

    "How hard can it be?"ER visit imminent

    When DIY is Acceptable

    Never.

    Seriously, there's no scenario where DIY pool table moving makes sense.

    Even if:

    • You're strong
    • Have friends
    • Own equipment
    • Have experience

    Still no. Too much risk.

    Pool Table Types

    Slate tables:Professional only. No exceptions.

    MDF/Wood tables:Still heavy (400-600 pounds)Still need disassemblyMaybe DIY (but why?)

    Outdoor tables:Lighter but awkwardPossible DIYStill risky

    Professional Advantages

    Beyond just moving:

    • Inspect for existing damage
    • Identify potential issues
    • Proper re-felting
    • Perfect leveling
    • Warranty on work
    • Peace of mind

    Value beyond transport

    Specific LA Challenges

    LA pool table moving issues:

    • Narrow hillside roads
    • Apartment stairs
    • Earthquake retrofitting
    • High-end tables common
    • Celebrity homes (perfection required)

    Local expertise matters

    Questions for Pros

    When hiring:

    1. "How many tables moved?"
    2. "Insurance coverage amount?"
    3. "Disassembly included?"
    4. "Re-felting available?"
    5. "Leveling guaranteed?"
    6. "References available?"

    Experienced pros answer confidently

    Red Flags

    Avoid if they say:

    • "We'll slide it"
    • "Disassembly not needed"
    • "Bring your friends to help"
    • "Extra for stairs" (after quote)
    • "Cash only"

    Find real professionals
