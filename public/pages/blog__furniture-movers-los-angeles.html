Los Angeles homes present unique challenges with narrow staircases, small elevators, and tight corners in Spanish-style architecture. SOS Moving specializes in furniture relocation, from IKEA assembly to piano moving, understanding that your furniture often holds more value than its price tag suggests.

    Why Furniture Requires Special Moving Services

    Standard movers treat furniture like boxes—load and go. Professional furniture movers understand each piece's requirements. Antiques need climate consideration. Leather requires breathing space. Glass components demand special packing. Assembly knowledge prevents damage during breakdown.

    Common furniture moving failures:Forcing pieces through tight spaces causes frame damage. Improper lifting strips veneer and joints. Wrong truck placement creates crushing. Missing hardware makes reassembly impossible. Scratches and dents from poor protection.

    Professional difference:Measuring doorways before attempting passage. Disassembly when necessary, not forced fitting. Proper weight distribution in truck. Cataloging hardware and components. Protection beyond simple blankets.

    Types of Furniture We Move

    Standard household furniture:Bedroom sets requiring careful disassembly. Living room sectionals navigating corners. Dining tables protecting leaves and surfaces. Office furniture maintaining adjustment mechanisms. Outdoor furniture preventing weather damage.

    Specialty pieces ($200 additional at SOS Moving):

    • Pianos: Uprights, baby grands, full grands
    • Pool tables: Slate protection crucial
    • Safes: Weight distribution essential
    • Armoires: Often won't fit anywhere
    • Antiques: Insurance documentation required

    IKEA and assembly furniture:Particle board requires gentle handling. Missing one screw ruins everything. Photography during disassembly essential. Instructions often lost or unclear. Reassembly expertise saves hours.

    LA's Unique Furniture Moving Challenges

    Architectural obstacles:Spanish-style homes with arched doorways limit height. Craftsman houses feature narrow hallways. Mid-century modern has floor-to-ceiling glass. Beach houses deal with sand and stairs. Downtown lofts have freight elevator requirements.

    Neighborhood-specific issues:Hollywood Hills driveways require hand-carrying. Venice walk-streets prohibit truck access. Beverly Hills demands white-glove service. Downtown high-rises need COI and scheduling.

    Furniture Protection Techniques

    Basic protection (included in standard service):Moving blankets wrap all surfaces. Shrink wrap secures blankets and drawers. Corner protectors for sharp edges. Furniture pads between stacked items.

    Enhanced protection for valuable pieces:Custom crating for irreplaceable items. Climate-controlled transport options. GPS tracking for high-value loads. Photography documenting condition. Additional insurance riders available.

    DIY Furniture Moving Dangers

    Injury risks:Back injuries from improper lifting top ER visits. Crushed fingers/toes common with heavy pieces. Hernias from straining frequent. Falls carrying items on stairs. Long-term damage from one wrong move.

    Property damage:Walls gouged by furniture corners ($500+ repairs). Floors scratched by dragging ($1,000+ refinishing). Doorframes cracked forcing pieces ($300+ each). Stairs damaged beyond patching. Ceiling fans destroyed by tall items.

    Furniture damage:Legs snap from lateral pressure. Veneer peels from moisture exposure. Joints separate permanently. Glass shatters from flexing. Mechanisms break from wrong angles.

    Preparing Furniture for Movers

    One week before:Photograph all pieces for documentation. Empty all drawers and compartments. Clean thoroughly (easier now than later). Identify pieces needing disassembly. Locate assembly instructions if available.

    Day before:Remove fragile decorations and hardware. Secure loose components with tape. Clear pathways for carrying routes. Measure tight spaces if concerned. Separate items needing special handling.

    Moving day:Point out valuable/fragile pieces. Discuss concerns about specific items. Provide assembly instructions found. Keep hardware bags labeled. Stay available for questions.

    Special Considerations by Furniture Type

    Leather furniture:Can't be plastic wrapped (needs breathing). Temperature sensitivity during transport. Conditioning recommended post-move. Professional cleaning if stored. Extra padding at pressure points.

    Antiques and heirlooms:Document value for insurance. Consider professional appraisal first. Climate control may be necessary. Restoration contacts ready if needed. Extra time for careful handling.

    Modern/designer furniture:Often harder to move than antiques. Unusual shapes don't stack well. Replacement parts impossible to find. Original packaging ideal if kept. Authentication documents travel separately.

    Mattresses and upholstered items:Require protective bags absolutely. Moisture damage happens quickly. Bed bugs spread without protection. Storage needs ventilation planning. Professional cleaning post-storage recommended.

    Insurance and Valuation

    Basic coverage (included):$0.60 per pound per item standard. $100 couch weighing 50 lbs = $30 coverage. Obviously inadequate for valuable pieces.

    Full value protection:Costs 1-2% of declared value. Covers repair or replacement. Required documentation of value. Deductibles may apply. Pre-existing damage excluded.

    Separate valuable items:Consider separate art/antique policy. Document everything extensively. Professional appraisals recommended. Move highest value pieces yourself. Keep irreplaceable items close.
