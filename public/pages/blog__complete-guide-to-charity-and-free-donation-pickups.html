    One of the best contributions one can make to humanity is to perform charitable acts & donate generously. Moreover, it’s an excellent opportunity to chuck out things that no longer serve any purpose in your life, especially when you move to a new place. But, most importantly, your donations can make a positive impact on people’s lives & also help those in need.

    However, sorting & packing your things is challenging when you’ve got a long to-do list to prepare before moving to your new home. While carrying them to a charity can be a hassle as it needs effort, attention, & time. Luckily, a few donation organizations that pick up your things for free so that you can focus on your moving process. If you’re looking for such charities & a few donation tips, here’s a comprehensive charity guide to making your life easier.

    Making Donations: What You Need to Know?

    There’s no hard-and-fast rule regarding the concept of giving back. But practicing a little generosity is a good habit & you can think of it as a way of paying it forward to your community. So, if you’re figuring out the details to make charitable donations, here’s what you need to know.

    • Ideally, Your Donations Should Be “Gently Used”
    • Your new, unused, or gently used furniture may not serve your needs anymore, but it can create joy for someone else. Since gently used items are mostly in good condition and resaleable, they have great potential to give back & serve communities.
    • Your Donations Could Be Tax-Deductible
    • Any donation, whether money, furniture, or other goods, is tax-deductible. All you have to do is get an itemized receipt. You’ll need some records, so you must secure your documents in a safe folder & use them to make the required tax deductions.
    • Most Charities Will Pick Up Your Donations for Free
    • The best part about donating your furniture to a charity is that some organizations provide free pickup service near your recommended areas. You can avail of their free service by visiting their website or contacting their local branch.
    • Not Every Item Will Be Accepted
    • There are some donation requirements that you need to follow before making charitable donations. Each non-profit organization has a different requirement list of acceptable items. For example, some of them may not accept stained or damaged items. When donating your furniture via a charitable organization, ensure that you qualify for their requirements for acceptable items.
    • What Do You Need to Know About Donating Furniture?
    • Are you planning to upgrade your home or office? Are you looking for donation organizations that pick up your furniture for free? The charities listed below can help you perform a little spring cleaning by taking your small furnishings & donating them for good.
    • When donating your furniture, it’s a good idea to ask these organizations if they accept large and small pieces of furniture. Some organizations do accept furnishings like coffee tables, office chairs, headboards, and more. At the same time, some may not accept heavy items like a sofa set, bedroom set, or dining table. That’s why you must contact the non-profit organization before making charitable donations.
    • What Do You Need to Know About Donating Books?
    • When donating your books, examine their condition first to check whether they aren’t torn or molded. They should also create value for other people in your community & don’t contain outdated information. If it’s a textbook or an encyclopedia, ensure with your organization that it’s recyclable or not or even worth donating to charity.
    • What Do You Need to Know About Donating Clothing?
    • Several people donate clothing in bulk as it’s the most common form of donation. However, it also depends on the needs of the people you’re donating to. Luckily, most charities in our list accept clothes, jewelry, belts, bags, shoes, and other accessories to help those in need. They will also sort through your torn, damaged, or stained clothes as they accept slightly used clothing that is clean & safe to use.

    How to Decide What You Should Keep, Donate, or Trash?

    If an object holds sentimental value or makes you happy, you should keep it.

    If it’s in good condition and doesn’t add any value to your life, donating seems more reasonable. However, if it’s damaged & doesn’t serve any purpose to you, it’s better to throw it away.

    Need help? See this decluttering guide to make the right decision about your furniture & other items.

    Donating Do’s and Don’ts

    Do’s

    • Make sure to check the condition of your item first;
    • If you’re donating clothes, wash & clean them properly;
    • Disinfect every item before making donations;
    • Avoid donating children’s items that can cause choking;
    • If it’s an electronic appliance, test it first.

    Don’ts

    • Donate any hazardous item, such as makeup or cleaning tools;
    • Donate broken, torn, or expired items;
    • Include outdated or banned stuff that also doesn’t meet the safety standards;
    • If you’re planning to drop off your item instead, make sure that the person or organization you’re donating to knows it.

    Quick Tips and Ideas for Donation Pickups

    1. Choose Items That Are Worth Donating
    2. It’s essential to choose items that are easy to recycle or of some use. They don’t have to be brand new, but they should be clean, safe, and defect-free.
    3. Look For Responsible Disposal Methods
    4. If your items aren’t in good condition, you can also look for other ways to dispose of them. For instance, you can send your old linen clothes to a local animal shelter, as they accept linen towels & stained clothes for different purposes.
    5. Do Some Research Before Choosing Your Charity
    6. Every charity has a different way of making an impact. You can learn about their values and methods first either from their website or by visiting them personally. It will help you make the right decision for your donations.
    7. Look for an Organization in Your Area
    8. You can schedule a pickup date if you have already researched the organizations that will pick up donations for free near you. But if you still need to figure out one, it’s better to look for a local organization in your area for a quick pickup schedule.
    9. Decide Honestly
    10. Before making your donations, evaluate your options carefully & then decide whether or not you should donate an item. If you’re holding onto a thing that doesn’t bring value or joy, it’s time to let it go.
    11. Get Your Children Involved
    12. Let your children learn these little acts of kindness to practice the same when they grow up. It’s good to involve them as much as possible so they can understand the concept of giving back.
    13. Preparations: Clean and Mend the Items
    14. If it’s furniture or any gently used item, you should leave them uncovered for inspection. However, if your charity organization has a special request, you can pack your stuff before the pickup date. Also, it’s essential to clean all the items first & check the condition of your items before making the required donation.
    15. Schedule Your Donation Pickup in Advance
    16. Once you’ve decided to move or donate your items, don’t wait any further. Instead, start researching your options & schedule a pickup date in advance to avoid any last-minute confusion.
    17. Ask for a Receipt
    18. For a tax-deductible charity, remember to ask for a donation receipt from your charity organization against the donations made.

    Donating FAQs

    How Do I Schedule a Donation Pickup?

    You can go through our How to Schedule Your Donation Pickup section.

    Where Can I Donate Furniture?

    Every charity organization has different requirements and criteria for accepting donations. For example, many accept small furnishings like coffee tables, chairs, etc. So, you’ll have to evaluate your options by contacting them to see which organization will accept your furniture.

    What Furniture Donations Are Typically Accepted?

    Generally, these organizations accept furniture items like tables, chairs, couches, bedding frames, mattresses, bookcases, office furniture, lamps, and other small items.

    What Is Not Available for Pickup?

    Large items like refrigerators, washers, and dryers are not available for pickup as they are challenging to lift and carry around by one person. Moreover, any organization will not accept any hazardous or broken item that doesn’t meet the safety standards.

    What Condition do My Items Need to be in for Donation?

    Your items should be in good condition that is resaleable & reusable by other people. That’s why most organizations accept gently used clothing or furniture for donations.

    Are My Donations Tax-deductible?

    Yes, all donations made to charities are tax-deductible, so make sure to take your receipt & save it in a safe place. You can read more about it here.

    Who Are My Donations Helping?

    It depends on the organization and your decision. For example, if you support a “charity by cause,” it will work on that particular cause.

    Where Can I Donate Clothes Besides Goodwill?

    You can view our charity list to donate gently used or unused clothes. For additional options, visit here.

    Does Goodwill Pick Up Donations?

    Yes, it does offer free pick-up for your donations. In addition, you can schedule an appointment time on their website.

    Who Will Pick Up a Donated Piano?
