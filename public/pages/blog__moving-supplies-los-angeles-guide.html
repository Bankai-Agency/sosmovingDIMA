Moving Supplies in Los Angeles: Complete Guide to Boxes and Packing Materials

Moving Supplies in Los Angeles: Complete Guide to Boxes and Packing Materials

Last Updated: 
Monday, November 3, 2025

Table of Contents

    The average LA move requires 60-100 boxes, 10 rolls of tape, 20 pounds of packing paper, and countless other supplies that add $300-600 to your moving budget—unless you know where to look and what you actually need. Most people overbuy some items while running out of essentials mid-pack. SOS Moving sees the aftermath of poor supply planning daily: crushed boxes, damaged items, and frantic last-minute store runs. This guide details exactly what supplies you need, where to find them cheap (or free) in Los Angeles, and professional tricks that save money.

    Essential Moving Supply Checklist

    Boxes you'll actually need:

    • Small boxes (1.5 cubic ft): Books, tools, canned goods
    • Medium boxes (3.0 cubic ft): Everything else
    • Large boxes (4.5 cubic ft): Linens, pillows, lampshades
    • Wardrobe boxes: Hanging clothes, curtains
    • Dish pack boxes: Dishes, glassware, fragile items
    • Mirror/picture boxes: Artwork, mirrors, flat screens

    Packing materials:

    • Packing paper: 5-10 lbs per room
    • Bubble wrap: 100 ft minimum
    • Packing peanuts: Skip them (messy, ineffective)
    • Moving blankets: Rent or buy 12-20
    • Plastic wrap: 2-3 rolls
    • Tape: 1 roll per 10 boxes

    Tools and supplies:

    • Box cutter/scissors: Multiple pairs
    • Permanent markers: Black and colored
    • Labels/stickers: Color-coding system
    • Dolly/hand truck: Rent if not owning
    • Furniture pads: Protect floors
    • Mattress bags: All bed sizes
    • Zip-lock bags: Hardware storage
    • Trash bags: Multiple uses

    How Many Boxes Do You Really Need?

    By home size (average):

    • Studio apartment: 20-30 boxes
    • 1-bedroom: 30-50 boxes
    • 2-bedroom: 50-75 boxes
    • 3-bedroom: 75-100 boxes
    • 4-bedroom: 100-150 boxes

    By room (typical):

    • Kitchen: 15-25 boxes
    • Bedroom: 10-15 boxes
    • Living room: 10-15 boxes
    • Bathroom: 3-5 boxes
    • Garage: 10-20 boxes
    • Office: 10-15 boxes

    The 80% rule: Whatever you estimate, add 20%. Running out mid-pack ruins everything.

    Free Moving Boxes in Los Angeles

    Liquor stores: Best free boxes anywhere. Strong, uniform size, handles. Call ahead, mornings best. Ralph's, Trader Joe's, BevMo gold mines.

    Grocery stores: Banana and apple boxes excellent. Ask produce department early morning. Whole Foods, Gelson's, Sprouts helpful.

    Restaurants: Fry oil boxes incredibly strong. Morning before lunch prep. McDonald's, fast food reliable.

    Office buildings: Copy paper boxes perfect for books. Check with building management. Downtown/Century City best.

    Craigslist/NextDoor: People giving away boxes post-move. Must act fast. Quality varies wildly.

    Facebook Groups: "Free Stuff LA" groups. "Buy Nothing" neighborhoods. Moving groups exchanges.

    Professional Packing Material Tips

    Packing paper vs newspaper:Newspaper stains everything black. Not worth saving $30. Buy clean packing paper.

    Bubble wrap alternatives:Towels, linens, clothes work. Save bubble wrap for truly fragile. $55/roll adds up quickly.

    Wardrobe box economics:Buying: $15-25 each. SOS provides free (returned). Garbage bag method works too.

    Specialty boxes worth it:Dish packs with dividers: Yes. TV boxes: Sometimes. Wine boxes: Absolutely. Mattress bags: Essential.

    Tape truth:Cheap tape splits constantly. Buy quality packing tape. 3" width minimum. One roll per 8-10 boxes.

    DIY Packing Supply Hacks

    Suitcases as boxes: Already own them. Wheels for heavy items. Protected interior. Stack easily.

    Laundry baskets: Perfect for odd shapes. Easy carrying. Reusable immediately. No unpacking needed.

    Garbage bags uses:

    • Protect hanging clothes
    • Stuff pillows/linens
    • Last-minute panic packing
    • Protect furniture

    Towels and linens: Wrap everything fragile. Fill empty spaces. Protect furniture legs. No cost padding.

    Original boxes: Electronics and appliances. Perfect fit guaranteed. Resale value maintained. Instructions included.

    Supply Costs Breakdown

    Budget moving (DIY everything):

    • Boxes (used/free): $0-50
    • Paper/bubble wrap: $75-100
    • Tape and supplies: $50-75
    • Total: $125-225

    Standard moving (mix new/used):

    • Boxes (quality mix): $150-250
    • Packing materials: $100-150
    • Tools and supplies: $50-100
    • Total: $300-500

    Premium moving (all new):

    • Professional boxes: $300-500
    • Quality materials: $200-300
    • Specialty supplies: $100-200
    • Total: $600-1,000

    When to Get Supplies

    6 weeks before: Research prices and options. Start collecting free boxes. Buy tape and markers.

    4 weeks before: Purchase 75% of supplies. Start packing non-essentials. Organize packing station.

    2 weeks before: Get remaining supplies. Final box count. Emergency supply run.

    Week of: Last-minute essentials only. Focus on packing, not shopping. Have backup plan.

    Organizing Your Packing Station

    Designated packing area:Clear room or garage space. Table at comfortable height. All supplies within reach. Good lighting essential. Music/entertainment setup.

    Supply organization:Boxes flat until needed. Materials in bins/boxes. Markers always visible. Tape guns loaded. Labels pre-made.

    Efficiency system:Assembly line approach. One person boxes, one wraps. Label immediately. Stack completed boxes. Track inventory.

    Post-Move Box Management

    Box disposal options:Give away on Craigslist/Facebook. Recycle at center. Save for others. Return to U-Haul (if eligible). Break down immediately.

    LA recycling centers:S.A.F.E. Centers throughout city. Blue bin if broken down. Bulk pickup available. Check city website.

    Paying it forward:Post in moving groups. Neighborhood apps. Help others save. Good karma earned.

    Supply Mistakes to Avoid

    Overbuying specialty boxes: Most things fit standard boxes. Specialty boxes expensive. Often unnecessary.

    Underbuying tape: Always need more. Buy bulk. Multiple locations.

    Wrong box sizes: Too big = too heavy. Too small = wasted space. Medium boxes most versatile.

    Waiting too long: Supplies run out weekends. Prices increase month-end. Stress increases exponentially.
