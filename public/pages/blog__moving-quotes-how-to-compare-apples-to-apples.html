Moving Quotes - How to Compare Apples to Apples

Moving Quotes - How to Compare Apples to Apples

Last Updated: 
Tuesday, January 27, 2026

Table of Contents

    Comparing moving quotes feels like comparing $800 to $1,200—obvious choice, right? Wrong. That cheaper quote excludes gas, insurance, materials, and overtime. The expensive quote includes everything plus wine and cheese. Neither quote means what it says. After analyzing thousands of LA moving quotes, here's how to compare actual apples to apples instead of apples to kumquats.

    Quote Format Variations

    Binding estimate:

    • Fixed price guaranteed
    • No surprises possible
    • Premium for certainty
    • Detailed inventory required

    Non-binding estimate:

    • "Not-to-exceed" pricing
    • Can go higher
    • Hourly rate basis
    • Cheaper initially

    Binding not-to-exceed:

    • Fixed maximum price
    • Can go lower
    • Best of both worlds
    • Rare but ideal

    Know which type you're comparing

    Hourly Rate Deception

    Company A quotes: $119/hourCompany B quotes: $159/hour

    But wait:

    • Company A: 3 movers, 6 hours = $2,142
    • Company B: 4 movers, 4 hours = $2,544

    Different crew sizes = different totals

    Always calculate total estimated cost

    Inclusion Variations

    Quote A ($800) includes:

    • Labor only
    • Basic tools
    • Nothing else

    Quote B ($1,200) includes:

    • Labor and truck
    • Full equipment set
    • Materials
    • Gas
    • Insurance

    $1,200 quote actually cheaper

    List what's included vs excluded

    Material Costs Hidden

    Free materials claims:

    • "Shrink wrap included" = $25 value
    • "Wardrobe boxes provided" = $50 value
    • "Floor protection" = $30 value

    Missing materials costs:

    • Blankets: $150 rental
    • Tape: $25
    • Dollies: $40
    • Tools: $30

    Add $200-300 to quotes without materials

    Gas Fee Variations

    Gas fee structures:

    • Flat fee: $30 (most common)
    • Per mile: $2/mile
    • Percentage: 5% of total
    • Included: No separate charge

    Same move, different fees:

    • 20 miles × $2 = $40
    • 5% of $1,000 = $50
    • Flat $30 = $30

    Normalize by calculating actual cost

    Travel Time Differences

    Portal-to-portal (better):

    • Clock starts at your door
    • Ends at destination
    • No surprises

    Dock-to-dock (cheaper initially):

    • Travel time billed separately
    • "Prep time" charged
    • Loading time extra

    Travel time can add 1-2 hours cost

    Insurance Variations

    Basic coverage:

    • $0.60/pound (worthless)
    • Often included free
    • Legal minimum only

    Enhanced coverage:

    • 1-2% of declared value
    • Actual replacement value
    • Deductible applies
    • Additional cost

    Factor insurance cost into total

    Crew Size Impact

    2-mover crew:

    • Takes 8 hours
    • $119 × 2 × 8 = $1,904

    3-mover crew:

    • Takes 5.5 hours
    • $139 × 3 × 5.5 = $2,293

    4-mover crew:

    • Takes 4 hours
    • $159 × 4 × 4 = $2,544

    More movers ≠ more expensive always

    Overtime Considerations

    Standard quotes assume 8 hours max

    Overtime scenarios:

    • After 8 hours: Time and a half
    • Saturdays: 25% premium
    • Sundays: 50% premium
    • Holidays: Double time

    Large moves often go overtime

    Ask about overtime policies

    Minimum Hour Requirements

    Common minimums:

    • 3 hours standard
    • 4 hours (if over 30 miles)
    • 5 hours (complex moves)

    Impact on small moves:

    • 2-hour move still costs 3-hour minimum
    • Efficiency penalties

    Factor minimums into calculations

    Additional Services

    Common add-ons:

    • Packing: $25-45/hour per person
    • Unpacking: $20-35/hour per person
    • Disassembly: $15-25/piece
    • Assembly: $20-35/piece
    • Piano moving: $150-300 extra

    Quote with and without add-ons

    Location Factors

    Difficult access charges:

    • Stairs: $25-50/flight
    • Long carry: $50-100
    • Elevator wait: $25/hour
    • Parking violations: Your cost

    Some quotes include, others don't

    Peak Season Adjustments

    Summer moving rates:

    • 25-40% higher
    • Weekend premiums
    • Holiday surcharges
    • Supply and demand

    Compare quotes from same time period

    Company Reputation Factor

    $800 quote from sketchy company:

    • Unlicensed operation
    • No insurance
    • Hidden costs appear
    • Damage likely
    • Real cost: $2,000+

    $1,200 quote from professionals:

    • Licensed/insured
    • Transparent pricing
    • No surprises
    • Quality service
    • Real cost: $1,200

    Cheap often costs more

    Payment Terms

    Payment structures:

    • Cash discount: 5-10%
    • Credit card fees: 3-5%
    • Deposit required: $50-200
    • Balance due: At delivery

    Factor payment costs into comparison

    Comparison Worksheet

    For accurate comparison:

    Quote 1: Company A

    • Base rate: $____
    • Crew size: ____ people
    • Estimated hours: ____
    • Materials included: Y/N
    • Gas fee: $____
    • Insurance: $____
    • Add-ons needed: $____
    • Total estimated: $____

    Quote 2: Company B(Same calculation)

    Quote 3: Company C(Same calculation)

    Red Flag Quotes

    Avoid quotes that:

    • Significantly below market
    • Lack detail
    • Require large deposits
    • Won't specify inclusions
    • Change during conversation
    • Pressure immediate signing

    If it seems too good to be true...

    Questions for Each Company

    Ask every mover:

    1. "What exactly is included?"
    2. "What are potential extra charges?"
    3. "How do you handle overtime?"
    4. "What's your damage policy?"
    5. "Can I see insurance certificate?"
    6. "Are there any other fees?"

    Compare answers, not just numbers

    Market Rate Reality

    LA moving rates 2026:

    • Budget: $119-139/hour
    • Standard: $149-169/hour
    • Premium: $179-199/hour

    Quotes way below = something missingQuotes way above = overpriced or premium

    The True Cost Revelation

    Cheapest quote problems:

    • Hidden fees emerge
    • Service quality poor
    • Damage common
    • Time overruns
    • Real cost higher

    Value-priced quotes:

    • Transparent pricing
    • Professional service
    • Predictable costs
    • Quality equipment

    Compare total experience, not just price

    Timeline Impact

    Rush jobs cost more:

    • Same-day: +50%
    • Next-day: +25%
    • Peak times: +30%

    Plan ahead saves money
