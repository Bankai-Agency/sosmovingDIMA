Weekend Moving in Los Angeles: Everything You Need to Know

Weekend Moving in Los Angeles: Everything You Need to Know

Last Updated: 
Saturday, October 4, 2025

Table of Contents

    Weekends are when 70% of LA moves happen. Everyone wants to move without taking time off work, leading to premium prices, limited availability, and logistical challenges. But weekend moving isn't always the wrong choice—sometimes paying extra makes sense for your situation. SOS Moving operates seven days a week across Los Angeles, giving us unique insight into weekend moving dynamics. This guide breaks down the real costs, benefits, and strategies for weekend relocations in LA.

    The Real Cost Difference: Weekday vs Weekend

    Let's start with actual numbers from SOS Moving's transparent pricing:

    Monday-Thursday rates:

    • 2 movers: $119/hour (cash) or $129/hour (card)
    • 3 movers: $159/hour (cash) or $169/hour (card)
    • 4 movers: $199/hour (cash) or $209/hour (card)

    Friday-Sunday rates:

    • 2 movers: $135/hour (cash) or $145/hour (card) - $16-26 more per hour
    • 3 movers: $175/hour (cash) or $185/hour (card) - $16-26 more per hour
    • 4 movers: $215/hour (cash) or $225/hour (card) - $16-26 more per hour

    For a typical 5-hour move, weekend premiums add $80-130 to your total cost. That's real money that could go toward packing supplies or settling-in expenses.

    Saturday vs Sunday: The Difference Matters

    Saturday - The Chaos Day:Saturday is peak moving day across LA. Every building's elevators are booked solid, parking is nightmare with multiple trucks competing, crews are rushed trying to fit in multiple jobs, and traffic is heavy with errands and activities. Popular areas like Santa Monica and Downtown LA become moving truck parking lots.

    Sunday - The Better Weekend Option:Sunday morning moves offer surprising advantages. Fewer moves scheduled means better crew attention, lighter traffic especially before 10 AM, easier parking in most areas, and some buildings don't count Sunday as "weekend" for moving restrictions. Religious observations mean certain neighborhoods are quieter.

    LA Traffic Patterns: Weekend Reality

    The myth that weekend traffic is always lighter needs examination by area:

    Areas with BETTER weekend traffic:

    • Downtown LA: No commuter traffic
    • Financial District: Empty on weekends
    • Burbank near studios: No production traffic
    • Business corridors along Wilshire

    Areas with WORSE weekend traffic:

    • Beach cities (Venice, Manhattan Beach): Tourist chaos
    • Hollywood: Nightlife and tourism
    • Shopping areas (Grove, Century City): Retail traffic
    • Near theme parks and attractions

    Best weekend windows:

    • Saturday: 6-9 AM before errands start
    • Sunday: 6 AM-noon before beach traffic
    • Avoid: Saturday 11 AM-6 PM, Sunday 2-7 PM

    Building Restrictions and Weekend Moves

    Many LA buildings have specific weekend policies affecting your move:

    Luxury buildings often prohibit weekend moves entirely:Beverly Hills and West Hollywood high-rises frequently ban Saturday-Sunday moves. Reserved for resident quiet enjoyment. May allow Sunday but not Saturday. Check before booking or face fines.

    Buildings requiring advance notice:Most require 72-hour notice for weekend moves. Elevator reservations book weeks ahead for Saturdays. Security deposit may be higher for weekends. Some charge extra fees for weekend staff.

    Apartments with weekend advantages:Complexes with multiple entrances avoid bottlenecks. Garden-style apartments don't require elevators. Newer buildings have freight elevators reducing conflicts. Valley properties tend more weekend-friendly.

    When Weekend Moving Makes Sense

    Despite higher costs, weekend moves make sense for specific situations:

    Cannot take time off work:Using PTO for moving seems wasteful. Some jobs don't allow short-notice time off. Hourly workers lose more than moving premium. New job starting Monday requires weekend move.

    School considerations:Kids can help or stay with friends. No missed school days during year. Teachers moving during school year. College students between semesters.

    Coordination with others:Friends more available to help on weekends. Family can assist with kids/pets. Partner's work schedule conflicts. Synchronized move-out/move-in timing.

    Long-distance move logistics:Flying family out requires weekend timing. Hotel check-in/out aligns better. More time for drive without rushing. Interstate movers often deliver weekends only.

    Strategies to Save on Weekend Moves

    If you must move on weekend, minimize the premium:

    Book far in advance:Weekend spots fill 3-4 weeks out. Early booking sometimes gets better rates. More crew options available. Avoid last-minute desperation pricing.

    Consider Sunday over Saturday:Often same weekend rate but easier logistics. Some companies offer Sunday discounts. Better crew energy (rested from Saturday). Churches make morning traffic lighter.

    Start extremely early:6 AM starts avoid traffic and heat. Crews fresher and work faster. Parking easier before residents wake. Finish before afternoon chaos.

    Hybrid approach:Move boxes yourself Friday evening. Hire SOS Moving just for furniture. Reduces hourly needs on premium day. Storage unit staging spreads work.

    Negotiate with flexibility:Offer to be "second move" after morning job. Take "standby" rate if cancellation occurs. Accept smaller crew if available. Pay cash for best rate.

    Hidden Weekend Costs Beyond Moving

    Utility connections:Many utilities don't connect weekends. Internet installers charge weekend premiums. Emergency connection fees if needed immediately. May need hotel if no power/water.

    Banking and services:Banks closed for address changes. DMV closed for registration needs. Post office closed for mail issues. Doctor offices closed if records needed.

    Shopping and setup:Stores crowded making errands take longer. Delivery services surge pricing on weekends. Restaurant delivery takes longer. Hardware stores packed with DIY crowds.

    Alternative Solutions to Weekend Moving

    Take one strategic day off:Wednesday or Thursday moves save most money. One PTO day often costs less than weekend premium. Easier to coordinate all services. Better experience overall with less stress.

    Split the move:Move essentials after work Friday. Complete move Saturday morning only. Reduces weekend hours needed. Local movers can handle partial moves.

    Negotiate work flexibility:Ask for late start after morning move. Work from home during move week. Comp time instead of weekend move. Some employers offer moving days.

    Use professional packers:Packing services during weekday. You just supervise on weekend. Reduces total weekend hours. Often offsetts premium with efficiency.

    The Bottom Line on Weekend Moving

    Weekend moving in Los Angeles costs more and comes with logistical challenges, but sometimes life demands it. The $80-130 premium for typical moves might be worth avoiding work complications or coordinating family help. Sunday moves offer better value than Saturday if you have flexibility. Early morning starts on either day improve the experience significantly.

    The key is honest cost-benefit analysis. If one vacation day saves $150 and reduces stress, take it. If work absolutely prevents weekday moving, book early and prepare thoroughly. Weekend moving isn't inherently bad—it's just important to understand what you're paying for and how to maximize value within those constraints.
