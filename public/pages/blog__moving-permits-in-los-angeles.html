Moving Permits in Los Angeles

Moving Permits in Los Angeles

Last Updated: 
Saturday, December 27, 2025

Table of Contents

    That perfect parking spot in front of your building? On moving day, it'll have three cars that haven't moved since 2019. Without proper moving permits in Los Angeles, you're choosing between $93 parking tickets every hour or hauling furniture three blocks. After watching thousands struggle with LA's permit maze, here's your complete guide to legal moving day parking.

    The Permit Reality

    What you actually need:

    • Temporary No Parking signs
    • 72-hour advance posting
    • $40 per sign
    • Usually need 2-4 signs
    • Total: $80-160

    What they give you:Legal right to reserve street parking. Maybe. If people respect signs. Which they won't. But you can call parking enforcement.

    Success rate: 60% on good days

    How to Get Permits

    Option 1: Online (Best)Website: ladotparking.org

    • Create account
    • Select location
    • Choose dates/times
    • Pay online
    • Print signs
    • Post 72 hours early

    Option 2: In PersonHollywood: 6501 Fountain AveDowntown: 201 N Los Angeles StValley: 6262 Van Nuys Blvd

    • Bring cash or card
    • Wait in line
    • Same day service

    Processing time: Immediate if in person, 1-2 days online

    The 72-Hour Rule

    Legal requirement:Signs must be posted 72 hours before enforcement begins.

    Example timeline:

    • Moving Saturday 8am
    • Post signs Wednesday 8am
    • Enforcement starts Saturday

    Common mistake:Posting Friday night for Saturday morning. Invalid. Cars won't be towed.

    Documentation:Photograph signs with timestamp. Prove proper posting.

    What Permits Actually Do

    They provide:

    • Legal basis for towing
    • Parking enforcement response
    • Some deterrent effect
    • Peace of mind

    They don't:

    • Guarantee empty spaces
    • Force immediate towing
    • Override film permits
    • Work without enforcement

    Reality check: You'll still deal with problems

    Enforcement Process

    If cars remain:

    1. Call parking enforcement: (213) 485-4184
    2. Officer arrives (maybe 30-60 minutes)
    3. Tickets issued first
    4. Tow truck called (another 30-60 minutes)
    5. Cars removed (hopefully)

    Total time: 2-3 hours if lucky

    Pro tip: Call enforcement at 6am moving day

    Special Zones and Restrictions

    Permit parking districts:Can't override resident permits. Residents park anyway.

    Street cleaning days:Great for moving! Already no parking. Time it right.

    Meters:Permits override meters. Cover meters with signs.

    Red zones:Cannot get permits. Ever. Don't try.

    Film shoot zones:Hollywood wins. Your permit loses. Check FilmLA.com

    Neighborhood Specific Issues

    West LA:Permits respected more. Enforcement responsive.

    Hollywood:Film shoots override everything. Tourists ignore signs.

    Downtown:Multiple permits needed (city and building). Enforcement quick.

    Valley:More space, less needed. Still recommended.

    Beach cities:Summer impossible. Tourists don't care.

    Building Coordination

    Many buildings require:

    • Their own permits
    • Management approval
    • Security coordination
    • Specific zones only

    Apartments/condos:Check if building has designated moving zones. Might not need street permits.

    High-rises:Loading dock usually. Street permits for overflow.

    When Permits Aren't Enough

    Situations requiring more:

    • Narrow streets (might need street closure)
    • Heavy equipment (crane permits)
    • Multiple trucks
    • Commercial buildings
    • Historic districts

    Additional permits:

    • Street closure: $500+
    • Sidewalk use: $200+
    • Lane closure: $1,000+
    • Crane permits: Complex

    Film Shoot Conflicts

    LA reality:Film shoots get priority. Always.

    Check before posting:

    • FilmLA.com
    • Neighborhood posts
    • Yellow signs

    If conflict:

    • Negotiate with location manager
    • Find alternate dates
    • Accept defeat sometimes

    Compensation:Sometimes production pays for inconvenience. Rarely.

    Permit Alternatives

    Private parking:

    • Rent neighbor's driveway: $50-100
    • Church parking lots: Sometimes free
    • School lots (weekends): Ask permission
    • Business lots (Sundays): Negotiate

    Loading zones:

    • 30-minute limit usually
    • Multiple trips required
    • Tickets if overstay

    Double parking:

    • Guaranteed ticket: $93
    • But sometimes necessary
    • Have someone stay with truck

    Cost-Benefit Analysis

    Permit costs:

    • Signs: $80-160
    • Time to post: 1 hour
    • Enforcement calls: Frustration

    No permit costs:

    • Tickets: $93-300
    • Extended move time: 2+ hours
    • Extra labor costs: $200+
    • Stress: Immeasurable

    Permits always worth it.

    Common Violations

    Why permits fail:

    • Posted less than 72 hours
    • Wrong dates/times
    • Incorrect addresses
    • Signs removed (theft/wind)
    • Improper posting height

    Results:

    • Unenforceable
    • No refund
    • Wasted money
    • Parking chaos

    Weekend vs Weekday

    Weekday permits:

    • Easier enforcement
    • Business hours response
    • More respect
    • Commuters problem

    Weekend permits:

    • Less enforcement available
    • Neighbors home (cars present)
    • More recreational parking
    • Different challenges

    Multi-Unit Buildings

    Coordinating multiple moves:

    • Reserve entire block
    • Share costs with neighbors
    • Coordinate timing
    • Management assistance

    Common areas:

    • One permit set
    • Building posts signs
    • Shared expense
    • Better success

    The Appeal Process

    If you get towed despite permits:

    • Document everything
    • Photos essential
    • Keep permit receipts
    • File complaint
    • Seek reimbursement

    Success rate: 50% if documented well

    Digital Evolution

    New systems coming:

    • Digital permits (pilot program)
    • QR code verification
    • Real-time enforcement
    • Better success rates

    Current reality:Still paper signs and hope

    Emergency Permits

    Last-minute needs:In-person only. No 72-hour requirement for emergencies (eviction, disaster).

    Requirements:

    • Proof of emergency
    • Higher fees sometimes
    • Limited enforcement

    Professional Mover Insights

    What we see:

    • 50% of customers don't get permits
    • Regret it always
    • Adds hours to move
    • Increases costs significantly

    Our recommendation:Always get permits. Always.

    Permit Checklist

    Two weeks before:

    • Check FilmLA for conflicts
    • Apply for permits online

    One week before:

    • Pick up/print signs
    • Buy posting materials

    72 hours before:

    • Post signs properly
    • Photograph proof
    • Notify neighbors

    Moving day:

    • Call enforcement early
    • Have backup plan
