Cross Country Movers - Coast to Coast Planning

Cross Country Movers - Coast to Coast Planning

Last Updated: 
Thursday, December 18, 2025

Table of Contents

    Cross country moving from Los Angeles means your belongings travel 2,000-3,000 miles through deserts, mountains, and weather you're not experiencing. This isn't just a longer local move—it's a complex logistics operation where timing, communication, and planning determine whether you're reunited with your stuff in a week or wondering where it went. Here's your complete coast-to-coast moving guide from LA to anywhere.

    Cross Country Moving Timeline

    LA to East Coast typical delivery:

    • New York: 10-14 days
    • Florida: 10-12 days
    • Boston: 12-16 days
    • DC: 10-14 days
    • Chicago: 7-10 days

    Why so long?

    • Your shipment joins others
    • Driver mandatory rest periods
    • Multiple pickups/deliveries
    • Weather delays possible
    • 2,800 miles takes time

    Express service (costs 50% more):

    • Dedicated truck
    • Direct route
    • 5-7 days typically

    Weight-Based Pricing Reality

    Cross country costs based on:

    • Total weight (pounds)
    • Distance (miles)
    • Season (summer higher)
    • Services (packing extra)

    Average weights:

    • Studio: 2,000-3,000 lbs
    • 1-bedroom: 3,000-5,000 lbs
    • 2-bedroom: 5,000-8,000 lbs
    • 3-bedroom: 8,000-12,000 lbs
    • 4-bedroom: 12,000-16,000 lbs

    Cost per pound: $0.50-0.80 average

    LA to NYC example:6,000 lbs × $0.65 = $3,900 basePlus fuel, insurance, extras = $5,000 total

    The Consolidation Process

    How it works:

    1. Your items loaded first
    2. Separator wall installed
    3. Next customer loaded
    4. Repeat until full
    5. Cross-country journey
    6. Reverse delivery order

    Your stuff shares space with 2-4 other shipments.

    Pros:

    • Cheaper than dedicated
    • Environmentally better

    Cons:

    • Longer delivery window
    • Multiple handling
    • Coordination complexity

    Cross Country Planning Checklist

    8 weeks before:

    • Research companies
    • Get 3-5 estimates
    • Check USDOT numbers
    • Read actual contracts

    6 weeks before:

    • Book your mover
    • Confirm dates
    • Start decluttering
    • Order supplies

    4 weeks before:

    • Begin packing non-essentials
    • Change address USPS
    • Notify utilities
    • Arrange car transport

    2 weeks before:

    • Confirm pickup date
    • Pack systematically
    • Prepare inventory
    • Banking changes

    1 week before:

    • Final confirmations
    • Pack essentials separately
    • Clean out appliances
    • Prepare payment

    What Can't Cross Country

    Prohibited items:

    • Hazardous materials
    • Flammables (paint, gas)
    • Ammunition
    • Perishable food
    • Plants (some states)
    • Firearms (special handling)

    Move yourself:

    • Important documents
    • Jewelry/cash
    • Medications
    • Electronics backup
    • Sentimental items
    • First week necessities

    Interstate Regulations

    Federal requirements:

    • Written estimate required
    • Bill of lading mandatory
    • Weight tickets provided
    • 110% rule applies
    • Rights pamphlet given

    Your protection:Can't charge over 110% of non-binding estimate at delivery. Balance due within 30 days.

    Communication During Transit

    What to expect:

    • Pickup confirmation
    • 24-hour delivery notice
    • Maybe one update
    • That's usually it

    What to ask for:

    • Driver's cell number
    • Dispatch contact
    • GPS tracking (rare)
    • Update schedule

    Reality: Your stuff disappears for 10 days. Normal but nerve-wracking.

    Delivery Day Planning

    Be prepared for:

    • Wide delivery window (8am-8pm)
    • Can't specify time
    • Must be available
    • Payment required
    • Check inventory immediately

    Before driver leaves:

    • Check every item
    • Note damage
    • Sign carefully
    • Document issues
    • Keep copies

    Cross Country Cost Breakdown

    LA to NYC (2,800 miles, 2-bedroom):

    • Base rate (7,000 lbs): $4,500
    • Packing service: $1,200
    • Insurance upgrade: $300
    • Long carry: $200
    • Storage if needed: $200/month
    • Total: $6,200-6,400

    LA to Miami (2,750 miles):Similar pricing, sometimes higher (less demand)

    LA to Chicago (2,000 miles):About 20% less than coasts

    Saving Money Strategies

    Reduce weight:Every pound costs. Sell heavy furniture, buy new.

    Off-season moving:October-February saves 20-30%

    Flexible dates:Let mover choose = discounts

    Pack yourself:Saves $1,000+ but risky

    Partial pack:You pack boxes, they pack fragile

    Insurance Decisions

    Basic coverage (included):$0.60 per pound per itemYour $2,000 TV weighs 50 lbs = $30 coverageWorthless.

    Full Value Protection:Costs 1% of declared value$50,000 shipment = $500Actually useful.

    Third-party insurance:Sometimes cheaperMore comprehensiveCheck your homeowners first

    Car Transportation

    Options from LA:

    • Drive yourself (adventure/exhausting)
    • Ship car ($1,200-1,800)
    • Sell and rebuy
    • Tow behind rental truck

    Car shipping timeline:Similar to household goods. Plan accordingly.

    Storage Considerations

    If home not ready:

    • Storage-in-transit (SIT)
    • First 30 days included sometimes
    • Then $150-300/month
    • Redelivery charges apply

    Alternative:Delay pickup until ready. Cheaper than storage.

    Common Cross Country Problems

    Delivery delays:Weather, breakdown, driver hours. Build buffer time.

    Weight disputes:Get certified weight tickets. Don't trust estimates.

    Damage claims:Document everything. File within 9 months.

    Communication gaps:Normal but frustrating. Daily calls won't help.

    Choosing Cross Country Movers

    Essential questions:

    1. "Are you a carrier or broker?"
    2. "What's your USDOT number?"
    3. "How many cross-country moves monthly?"
    4. "What's the delivery window?"
    5. "Binding or non-binding estimate?"

    Red flags:

    • Extremely low quotes
    • Large deposits required
    • No physical address
    • Won't provide references
    • Vague about timing

    Preparing for Pickup

    Day before:

    • Defrost freezer
    • Pack essentials bag
    • Prepare payment
    • Charge devices
    • Get cash for tips

    Pickup day:

    • Be ready at 7am
    • Everything packed
    • Clear pathway
    • Inventory ready
    • Documents copied

    Life Between Homes

    The gap period:You'll likely arrive before your stuff.

    Survival kit:

    • Air mattress
    • Week of clothes
    • Kitchen basics
    • Work necessities
    • Cleaning supplies

    Budget for:

    • Temporary furniture rental?
    • Eating out more
    • Missing item replacements

    Regional Considerations

    LA to Northeast:

    • Winter clothes needed
    • Humidity adjustment
    • Culture shock real

    LA to Southeast:

    • Humidity extreme
    • Different pace
    • Bug situation

    LA to Midwest:

    • Real winters
    • Friendlier pace
    • Cost savings significant

    The Emotional Journey

    Week 1: Excitement, living minimalWeek 2: Where's my stuff anxietyDelivery day: Christmas morning feelingWeek 3: Unpacking realityMonth 2: Finally settled

    It's a process. Be patient.
