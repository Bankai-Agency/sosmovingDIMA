Where to Get Free Moving Boxes in Los Angeles

Where to Get Free Moving Boxes in Los Angeles

Last Updated: 
Thursday, February 5, 2026

Table of Contents

    Free moving boxes in Los Angeles save $200-400 on your moving budget, but finding quality boxes requires strategy, timing, and realistic expectations. Between liquor stores, grocery chains, and online communities, LA offers dozens of free box sources. The challenge isn't finding boxes—it's finding clean, sturdy boxes that won't collapse mid-move. Here's your complete guide to scoring quality free moving boxes across Los Angeles.

    Liquor Stores (Best Overall Source)

    Why liquor stores work:

    • High box turnover
    • Sturdy construction (glass protection)
    • Variety of sizes
    • Regular delivery schedules
    • Managers often cooperative

    Best chains:

    • BevMo (large, clean boxes)
    • Total Wine (wine boxes perfect)
    • Local independent stores
    • Costco liquor (if accessible)

    Timing strategy:

    • Tuesday-Thursday mornings
    • After delivery trucks
    • Build relationships with managers
    • Call ahead before visiting

    Box quality: Excellent for books/heavy items

    Grocery Store Gold Mine

    Top grocery chains:

    • Ralph's (consistent supply)
    • Vons/Safeway (good variety)
    • Whole Foods (premium boxes)
    • Trader Joe's (smaller but sturdy)
    • Smart & Final (bulk boxes)

    Best departments:

    • Produce (banana boxes ideal)
    • Pharmacy (small, sturdy)
    • Cereal aisle restocking
    • Deli counter
    • Loading docks

    Approach:Ask managers, not clerks. Explain you're moving and offer to help break down boxes they'd discard anyway.

    Online Communities

    Facebook groups:

    • "Buy Nothing [Your Neighborhood]"
    • "LA Moving & Storage"
    • "Free Stuff Los Angeles"
    • Neighborhood-specific groups

    Craigslist free section:

    • Check "for sale > free"
    • Search "moving boxes"
    • Post "wanted" ads
    • Act fast on good listings

    NextDoor app:

    • Neighborhood networks
    • Recent movers giving away
    • Local recommendations
    • Trust factor higher

    Corporate Offices

    Office buildings offer:

    • Copy paper boxes (perfect size)
    • Clean condition
    • Regular availability
    • Professional relationships

    Best targets:

    • Law firms (document boxes)
    • Accounting offices (file boxes)
    • Real estate offices
    • Corporate headquarters

    Approach during lunch hours or after 5pm

    Electronics Stores

    What they discard:

    • TV boxes (large items)
    • Computer boxes (small electronics)
    • Appliance packaging
    • Display boxes

    Target stores:

    • Best Buy
    • Fry's Electronics
    • Local computer stores
    • Apple Store (rare but perfect)

    Timing: Delivery days and display changes

    Bookstores

    University bookstores:

    • UCLA Store
    • USC Bookstore
    • Community colleges
    • Semester changeovers

    Chain bookstores:

    • Barnes & Noble
    • Independent stores
    • Comic book shops

    Perfect for: Books and small items

    Restaurants and Bars

    Food service boxes:

    • Various sizes
    • Frequent turnover
    • Easy relationships
    • Night availability

    Considerations:

    • May have food residue
    • Grease stains possible
    • Check for cleanliness
    • Better for non-food items

    Moving Box Exchange Networks

    U-Haul Box Exchange:

    • Online platform
    • Local pickup
    • Verified sellers
    • Small fees sometimes

    Moving companies:

    • Recent customers donate
    • Call and ask
    • Build relationships
    • Seasonal availability

    Apartment Complexes

    Why they work:

    • Constant move-outs
    • Residents discard boxes
    • Property managers helpful
    • Variety of sizes

    Best locations:

    • Luxury complexes (better boxes)
    • Student housing (frequent turnover)
    • Corporate housing
    • Near colleges/universities

    Check dumpster areas (with permission)

    Retail Stores

    Department stores:

    • Macy's
    • Target
    • Nordstrom
    • TJ Maxx/Marshall's

    Specialty retailers:

    • Home Depot/Lowe's
    • Office supply stores
    • Clothing stores
    • Shoe stores

    Ask customer service or receiving departments

    Warehouse Stores

    Costco/Sam's Club:

    • Large, sturdy boxes
    • Bulk packaging discarded
    • Membership required for access
    • Weekend restocking

    Business supply stores:

    • Staples
    • Office Depot
    • Restaurant supply stores

    Timing Your Hunt

    Best days:

    • Tuesday-Thursday (delivery days)
    • Weekend mornings (restocking)
    • End of month (inventory changes)

    Worst times:

    • Mondays (boxes gone)
    • Late afternoons (picked over)
    • Holiday weeks (limited staff)

    Box Quality Assessment

    Good box indicators:

    • No tears or holes
    • Strong corners
    • Dry condition
    • Reasonable size
    • Original tape removed

    Avoid boxes with:

    • Water damage
    • Grease stains
    • Pest activity
    • Collapsed corners
    • Strong odors

    Test by lifting—should feel solid

    Free Box Hunting Tips

    Preparation:

    • Bring measuring tape
    • List needed sizes
    • Have vehicle ready
    • Bring help for loading

    Etiquette:

    • Be polite and grateful
    • Explain your situation
    • Offer to help break down boxes
    • Don't take more than needed
    • Leave area clean

    What You'll Still Need to Buy

    Specialty boxes for:

    • Wardrobe hanging clothes
    • Dish packs (fragile protection)
    • Mirror/picture frames
    • Mattress bags

    Supplies still required:

    • Quality packing tape
    • Bubble wrap
    • Packing paper
    • Markers for labeling

    Free boxes + purchased supplies = major savings

    Seasonal Considerations

    Best seasons:

    • Summer (moving season = more boxes)
    • Back-to-school (student moves)
    • End of year (office cleanouts)

    Challenging times:

    • Holiday seasons (reduced business)
    • Winter (fewer moves)
    • Beginning of year (limited supply)

    Alternative Free Sources

    Construction sites:

    • Appliance boxes
    • Material packaging
    • Ask site supervisors

    Events/conventions:

    • Display boxes
    • Shipping containers
    • Contact organizers

    Safety and Legal Notes

    Always:

    • Ask permission first
    • Don't trespass
    • Check building policies
    • Respect private property

    Never:

    • Take from private dumpsters
    • Ignore "No Trespassing" signs
    • Make messes
    • Argue with employees

    Maximize Your Free Box Success

    Free moving boxes in Los Angeles require effort but save hundreds of dollars. Quality sources exist throughout the city for those willing to invest time in hunting and relationship building. Start early, be flexible, and maintain realistic expectations.
