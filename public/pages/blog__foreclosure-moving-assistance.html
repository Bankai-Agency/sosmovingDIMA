Foreclosure Moving Assistance

Last Updated: 
Friday, December 26, 2025

Table of Contents

    The foreclosure notice arrives, and suddenly you have 30-60 days to leave a home you've lived in for years. The shame, stress, and overwhelming logistics make people freeze when action is needed most. After helping hundreds through foreclosure moves in Los Angeles, we know the resources available, the timeline reality, and how to protect what matters when everything feels lost.

    Understanding the Timeline

    Foreclosure isn't immediate:

    • Notice of Default: 90 days to cure
    • Notice of Sale: 21 days minimum
    • Eviction after sale: 30-60 days
    • Total process: 4-8 months typically

    You have more time than you think.

    Critical dates:

    • Auction date (home still yours until sold)
    • New owner notification
    • Cash for keys deadline
    • Eviction filing
    • Sheriff lockout date

    Each stage has different rights and options.

    Cash for Keys Programs

    What it is:Bank pays you to leave voluntarily, avoiding eviction costs.

    Typical offers:

    • $3,000-10,000 common
    • Sometimes up to $20,000
    • Negotiable often

    Requirements:

    • Leave by specific date
    • Property in good condition
    • No damage/stripping
    • Keys turned over

    This money funds your move and fresh start.

    Legal Rights During Foreclosure

    You still have rights:

    • Cannot be locked out without court order
    • Utilities cannot be shut off
    • Personal property is yours
    • Harassment is illegal
    • Time to retrieve belongings

    Tenant rights (if renting foreclosed property):

    • 90-day notice required
    • Lease may be honored
    • Relocation assistance possible
    • Cash for keys negotiable

    Document everything. Know your rights.

    Storage Solutions

    If you can't move everything immediately:

    Free/cheap storage options:

    • Friends/family garages
    • Church storage programs
    • Community storage shares
    • Negotiated storage with new owner

    Professional storage:

    • First month specials common
    • No credit check options
    • Month-to-month terms
    • Climate control for valuables

    Storage buying time while you figure out next steps.

    What to Save First

    Priority items:

    • All documents (birth certificates, SSN, tax records)
    • Medications
    • Family photos/videos
    • Computers/hard drives
    • Jewelry/valuables
    • Children's special items
    • Tools for work
    • Professional clothes

    Second priority:

    • Furniture you love
    • Kitchen essentials
    • Bedding
    • Seasonal clothes
    • Electronics

    Let go:

    • Old furniture
    • Broken items
    • Unused belongings
    • Replaceable goods

    Housing Options

    Immediate/temporary:

    • Extended stay hotels
    • Room rentals
    • Staying with family
    • Shelter systems (last resort)

    Longer term:

    • Apartment with co-signer
    • Rent-by-week places
    • Mobile home parks
    • Shared housing

    Credit issues:

    • Look for private landlords
    • Offer larger deposit
    • Get recommendation letters
    • Explain situation honestly

    Emotional Support Resources

    This is traumatic. Get help:

    • National Suicide Prevention: 988
    • Crisis text line: Text HOME to 741741
    • NAMI helpline: 1-800-950-6264
    • Local support groups

    For families:

    • Explain to children honestly
    • Maintain routines
    • Seek counseling
    • Schools have resources

    No shame in needing help.

    Avoiding Scams

    Foreclosure attracts predators:

    Red flags:

    • Upfront fees for "rescue"
    • Deed transfer requests
    • Guaranteed loan modifications
    • Pressure tactics
    • Too-good-to-be-true offers

    Legitimate help is usually free.

    Verify everything:

    • HUD-approved only
    • Licensed attorneys
    • Established nonprofits
    • Government programs

    Moving Day Strategy

    If eviction scheduled:

    • Be packed before date
    • Remove everything valuable
    • Take photos of condition
    • Turn in keys properly
    • Avoid sheriff involvement

    Professional movers can:

    • Work with tight deadlines
    • Coordinate storage
    • Handle emotional situation
    • Provide discrete service
    • Offer payment plans

    Rebuilding After Foreclosure

    Credit recovery:

    • Takes 3-7 years
    • Not permanent
    • Rebuilding possible
    • Many have recovered

    Housing future:

    • FHA loans possible after 3 years
    • VA loans after 2 years
    • Renting gets easier with time
    • Co-signers help

    Career/income:

    • Not affected usually
    • May reduce stress
    • Fresh start opportunity
