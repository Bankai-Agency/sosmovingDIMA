## Why Pianos Require Specialists

**Weight distribution complexities:**Uprights carry weight high and back-heavy. Grands concentrate mass in cast-iron harp. Uneven weight creates tipping hazards. Standard dollies fail catastrophically. Legs support minimal lateral force.

**Internal damage risks:**Soundboard cracks from flexing frame. Hammers misalign from vibration. Tuning pins loosen from shock. Pedal mechanisms bend easily. Strings snap from sudden movement.

**Monetary and sentimental value:**Replacement costs $3,000-300,000. Restoration often exceeds purchase price. Family heirlooms irreplaceable. Professional instruments affect livelihoods. Insurance claims complex without specialists.

## The Professional Piano Moving Process

**Pre-move assessment:**Measure all pathways precisely. Check structural floor support. Identify turning radiuses required. Plan equipment needs specifically. Determine crew size necessary.

**Preparation phase:**Lock or secure keyboard lid. Remove music stands and decorations. Wrap in moving blankets completely. Secure blankets with strapping. Protect corners with padding.

**Moving uprights:**Tilt onto piano dolly carefully. Secure with heavy straps. Navigate with spotters always. Never lift by keyboard. Maintain vertical orientation mostly.

**Moving grands:**Remove legs and pedal lyre. Place on grand piano skid. Secure harp side down. Navigate on special dollies. Reassemble with precision.

## Preparing Your Piano for Moving

**Two weeks before:**Schedule professional tuning after move. Document current condition thoroughly. Photograph serial numbers clearly. Research destination placement. Clear pathways both locations.

**One week before:**Remove all items from piano. Clean thoroughly while accessible. Check for loose parts. Secure fallboard and lid. Gather any documentation.

**Moving day:**Point out piano immediately. Discuss any concerns openly. Provide special instructions clearly. Stay available for questions. Plan placement precisely.

## Piano Placement Considerations

**Ideal placement factors:**Interior walls prevent temperature fluctuations. Away from windows avoiding sun. Distance from heating/cooling vents. Level floor absolutely critical. Humidity control beneficial.

**Avoid placing near:**Exterior walls (temperature changes). Fireplaces (heat and dryness). Kitchens/bathrooms (humidity). Direct sunlight (fading/warping). High-traffic areas (accidents).
