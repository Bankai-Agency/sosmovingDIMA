## The Professional Packing Process

**Day before packing:**Team leader surveys home. Creates packing plan strategically. Orders appropriate materials. Discusses special concerns. Provides timeline estimate.

**Packing day sequence:**Start with non-essentials always. Room-by-room systematic approach. Fragiles get special attention. Everything labeled clearly. Inventory list created.

**Quality standards:**No box exceeds 50 pounds. Fragile items double-wrapped minimum. Heavy items in small boxes. Light items in large boxes. Custom solutions for odd shapes.

## Room-by-Room Packing Strategies

**Kitchen (most complex):**Dishes wrapped individually in paper. Glasses packed vertically, never flat. Appliances in original boxes ideally. Sharp knives secured safely. Liquids sealed and bagged.

**Bedroom challenges:**Clothing on hangers in wardrobe boxes. Drawers sometimes left intact. Jewelry requires special attention. Mattresses need protective bags. Electronics documented and wrapped.

**Living areas:**Electronics photographed before disconnection. Cables labeled and bagged together. Artwork wrapped and crated. Books spine-down in small boxes. Decoratives nested carefully.

**Garage/storage:**Hazardous materials identified and excluded. Tools organized in appropriate containers. Seasonal items grouped logically. Power equipment drained properly. Paint and chemicals left behind.

## Common Packing Mistakes We Prevent

**Weight distribution errors:**Books in large boxes (too heavy). Linens in small boxes (space waste). Mixed weight causing crushing. Bottom-heavy creating tipping. Overloaded boxes failing.

**Inadequate protection:**Single wrapping fragile items. Newspaper staining belongings. Insufficient padding between items. Empty spaces allowing shifting. Liquids unsealed leaking everywhere.

**Organization failures:**Random mixing room items. No labeling system used. Essentials buried deeply. Important documents packed away. No inventory maintained.
