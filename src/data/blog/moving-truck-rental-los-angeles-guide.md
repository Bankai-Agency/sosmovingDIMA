## Truck Sizes and What Fits

**Cargo Van (245 cubic feet):**

- Fits: Studio or dorm room- Length: 9-11 feet- Best for: Small loads, easy parking- MPG: 18-22- Daily cost: $50-70- Reality: Usually too small

**10-12 foot truck (380-450 cubic feet):**

- Fits: Studio to small 1-bedroom- Holds: 1-2 rooms maximum- Weight: 3,000-3,500 lbs- Best for: Partial moves, easy driving- Daily cost: $70-90- MPG: 12-14

**15-17 foot truck (750-850 cubic feet):**

- Fits: Large 1-bedroom to small 2-bedroom- Holds: 2-3 rooms comfortably- Weight: 4,500-6,000 lbs- Best for: Most apartment moves- Daily cost: $90-120- MPG: 10-12

**20-22 foot truck (1,000-1,200 cubic feet):**

- Fits: 2-3 bedroom apartment/house- Holds: 3-5 rooms- Weight: 8,000-10,000 lbs- Best for: Family moves- Daily cost: $120-150- MPG: 8-10

**26 foot truck (1,700 cubic feet):**

- Fits: 3-4 bedroom house- Holds: 5-7 rooms- Weight: 10,000-12,000 lbs- Best for: Large homes only- Daily cost: $150-200- MPG: 6-8

## Size Selection Strategy

**Rule of thumb:** 150-200 cubic feet per room. Add 10-15% for safety. Consider access limitations. Bigger isn't always better.

**Common sizing mistakes:**

**Too small (most common):**Studio renters getting cargo van. 2-bedroom in 15-footer. Forgetting garage/patio. Underestimating furniture volume.

**Too large (also problematic):**1-bedroom in 20-footer. Empty space causes shifting. Harder to drive/park. Wastes money significantly.

**Sweet spots:**Studio: 10-12 footer. 1-bedroom: 15-17 footer. 2-bedroom: 20 footer. 3-bedroom: 22-26 footer.

## Driving a Moving Truck in LA

**Before driving:**Adjust mirrors completely. Check clearance height. Test brakes empty. Understand blind spots. Practice parking briefly. Know your route.

**Key differences from cars:**Acceleration much slower. Braking distance doubles+. Turning radius huge. Wind affects significantly. No rearview mirror. Backing up terrifying.

**LA navigation tips:**Avoid rush hours absolutely (7-10 AM, 3-7 PM). Take wider streets. Skip narrow shortcuts. Check bridge clearances. Use truck routes. Plan parking ahead.

**Speed recommendations:**Surface streets: 5-10 under limit. Highways: Right lane only. Hills: Use lower gears. Turns: Very slow. Parking: Multiple attempts normal.

## Insurance Decisions

**Your existing coverage:**Check auto insurance first. Many exclude rental trucks. Credit cards vary wildly. Homeowners might help. Don't assume coverage.

**Rental insurance options:**Basic: Covers catastrophic only. Standard: Better but gaps. Premium: Most complete. None: Massive risk.

**What's typically NOT covered:**Overhead damage (very common). Cargo damage. Side mirror damage. Parking lot dings. Interior damage.

## One-Way vs Round-Trip

**Round-trip rentals:**Cheaper per day usually. Must return same location. Mileage adds up. Good for local. Time pressure returning.

**One-way rentals:**Higher daily rate. Mileage sometimes included. No return trip. Better for distance. Location flexibility.

**LA to popular cities (one-way):**
