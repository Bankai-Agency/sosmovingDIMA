## Factors That Contribute to the Cost of Move

### 1. Size of Your Move

The cost of your move is heavily influenced by your home size and the number of possessions.

For example, moving from a modestly equipped studio apartment will almost certainly cost less than moving from a 2- or 3-bedroom apartment. A three-bedroom apartment will likely have fewer belongings than a three-bedroom home with an attic, a garage, etc.

While the typical cost of moving is between $400 and $1,600, with houses costing more than apartments, you should still contact a local moving firm for a more accurate quotation.

### 2. Storage

If you need storage, the overall cost of your move will increase. Some businesses offer free storage. If your moving company does not offer this, consider factoring storage charges into your budget The higher the costs, the more stuff you can store.

### 3. Moving Charges

Moving companies typically estimate the cost of your move hourly. The average cost of movers per hour is often around $150. (This is for three movers.) This type of expenditure is more typical in local relocation than long-distance moves.

Prices by the hour will help you estimate how much the job will cost. The final amount will be determined by the size of your relocation, the travel distance, and the projected time required for movers to complete their work.

### 4. Liability Expenses

When traveling a long distance, you should purchase additional insurance from a third party insurance company. This form of security, however, will only increase the final cost of the relocation. Moving companies provide little liability coverage, which is insufficient. It is essential when moving valuable objects.

## DIY Move vs. Hiring Movers

While the cost of moving to Los Angeles can be steep, there are multiple ways to achieve your goal, such as hiring movers or opting for a DIY move. However, it would be wise first to consider the average cost beforehand and then move forward with your venture.

When it comes to hiring movers, you have a few options that will influence the cost of your move. The most expensive choice will almost certainly be a full-service move. On the other hand, a full-service move includes the rental of a truck, possession pick-up (including box loading), and delivery and unloading at your new home or storage facility. Put another way: if heavy lifting and packing aren’t your things, this is your safest bet. With a full-service relocation, you can genuinely relax knowing that your belongings are in capable hands.

On the other hand, if you are up for a challenge, you can opt for the DIY move, which will surely be a beyond-hectic procedure but comparatively less expensive, nonetheless.

## Long Distance Move

Earlier, we reviewed the hourly charges of a local, short-distance move. When hiring long-distance movers, it is customary to be charged a fixed amount. A moving company will provide you with a fixed charge for the cost of the relocation, whether you are moving locally or across the country. It is an estimate of the entire cost of the job, from packing the moving truck at your origin to unloading your belongings at your new home.

Long-distance transfers may take longer and be more complex logistically than short-distance trips. Depending on how far you’re traveling, a flat rate may be preferable to an hourly rate. Consider the distance, do some math, and then proceed. If we take moving from LA to SF, for instance, [your move to San Francisco will cost](https://sosmovingla.net/blog/how-much-does-it-cost-to-move-from-la-to-san-francisco/) nearly $1,400 -2,500, depending on various other factors.

## Additional Services
