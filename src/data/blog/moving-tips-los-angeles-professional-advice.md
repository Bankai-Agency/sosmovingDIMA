## Packing Tips That Actually Work

**1. Clothes as packing material:** Your clothes are free bubble wrap. Wrap dishes in t-shirts, cushion electronics with towels, protect frames with sweaters. Kills two birds, saves money.

**2. Leave clothes in dresser drawers:** Just wrap the whole dresser in plastic wrap. Drawers stay put, clothes stay folded, one less thing to pack/unpack.

**3. Photograph electronic setups:** Before disconnecting your TV/stereo/computer, photograph all connections. You'll thank yourself when reconnecting.

**4. Color-code by room:** Assign each room a color. Mark boxes and doorways with corresponding colored tape. Movers know instantly where everything goes.

**5. Pack plates vertically:** Like records in a crate, not stacked flat. They're less likely to break from weight pressure.

**6. Garbage bags for hanging clothes:** Slip bags over groups of hanging clothes from bottom up, tie at hangers. Poor man's wardrobe box.

**7. Keep hardware in labeled bags:** Tape furniture hardware to the furniture itself or in clearly labeled Ziploc bags. Nothing worse than mystery screws.

**8. Pack heavy items in small boxes:** Books in large boxes equal thrown-out backs. Use small boxes, even if it means more boxes.

**9. Fill empty spaces:** Socks in shoes, spices in pots, linens in washer drum. Every empty space is wasted money.

**10. Suitcases for heavy items:** They have wheels and handles designed for weight. Perfect for books, tools, canned goods.

## Money-Saving Moving Tips

**11. Move during off-peak times:** Monday-Thursday saves $16-26/hour with SOS Moving. Mid-month moves cost 20% less. October-February cheapest months.

**12. Sell before moving, buy after:** That old couch costs $300 to move. Sell for $200, buy better one for $400 at destination. Net savings plus upgrade.

**13. Tax deduction documentation:** Job-related moves may be deductible. Keep every receipt. Photo everything. Document timeline.

**14. Compare insurance options:** Basic coverage (60¢/lb) inadequate. Full-value protection costs 1-2% of value but covers actual replacement.

**15. Purge ruthlessly:** Average American moves 7,000 lbs of stuff they don't use. Every pound costs money. If you haven't used it in a year, don't move it.

**16. Use your own supplies:** Liquor stores have strong boxes free. Newspaper from recycling centers. Blankets you already own. Saves $200-500.

**17. Book early for better rates:** Last-minute desperation costs 50-100% more. Three weeks advance booking optimal.

**18. Measure large furniture first:** That couch that won't fit costs $300 to move, then $300 to return. Measure doorways at both locations.

## LA-Specific Moving Tips

**19. Reserve parking early:** LA parking enforcement doesn't care about your move. $73 tickets add up. Reserve spots 72 hours ahead.

**20. Know building requirements:** [Downtown high-rises](../../movers-downtown-los-angeles/index.html) require COI, elevator reservations, specific hours. Missing requirements delays everything.

**21. Plan for traffic reality:** "20 miles" in LA means 45-90 minutes depending on time/route. Morning moves beat afternoon traffic.

**22. Beach community challenges:** [Venice](../../venice-movers/index.html) and [Santa Monica](../../los-angeles-movers/santa-monica-movers/index.html) have tourist traffic, limited parking, walk-streets. Plan accordingly.

**23. Hill area strategies:** [Hollywood Hills](../../movers-hollywood/index.html) and [Silver Lake](../moving-to-silver-lake-los-angeles/index.html) require smaller trucks, extra crew for carrying. Costs more but necessary.

**24. Earthquake prep transfers:** Don't pack emergency kit. Keep accessible at both locations. Update for new address immediately.

**25. Weather rarely helps:** June gloom, October heat waves, winter rain. LA weather surprises. Have tarps ready.

## Damage Prevention Tips

**26. Double-box electronics:** Original box inside moving box with padding between. Extra protection worth it.

**27. Plastic wrap everything with drawers:** Dressers, filing cabinets, toolboxes. Drawers won't slide, contents won't spill.

**28. Stand mattresses on side:** Never flat where they can bend. Always vertical against truck wall. Use mattress bags.

**29. Remove legs from tables:** Every table leg is a breaking point. Remove when possible, tape to table bottom.

**30. Empty everything with liquid:** Seems obvious until your shampoo explodes over everything. Empty or seal in plastic bags.

**31. Protect floors both locations:** Cardboard, tarps, or ram board. Damage deposits depend on it. Scratched floors cost thousands.

**32. Wrap furniture corners:** Corner protectors or cardboard and tape. Most damage happens to corners and edges.

## Stress Reduction Tips

**33. Pack essentials box clearly marked:** First night items: toiletries, medications, chargers, toilet paper, tools, cash. Opens first, saves sanity.

**34. Keep important documents with you:** Don't pack birth certificates, passports, insurance papers, leases. Carry personally always.

**35. Take pictures of valuable items:** Before packing for condition documentation. Shows pre-existing damage, helps insurance claims.

**36. Number boxes and list contents:** "Kitchen stuff" helps nobody. "Kitchen #1: plates, bowls, cups" helps everyone.

**37. Pack room-by-room completely:** Don't jump around. Finish one room entirely before starting another. Sense of progress matters.

**38. Start with least-used rooms:** Garage, attic, guest room first. Daily-use rooms last. Maintains normal life longer.

## Moving Day Tips

**39. Be ready before movers arrive:** Everything packed, pathways clear, decisions made. Movers clock starts on arrival.

**40. Provide refreshments for crew:** Water, sports drinks, snacks. Happy movers work harder, care more. Worth $20 investment.

**41. Stay available but out of way:** Be there for questions, don't hover. Trust professionals to work.

**42. Check inventory carefully:** Note pre-existing damage on forms. Check off items as loaded/unloaded. Your receipt for everything.

**43. Tip appropriately:** $20-40 per mover standard for good service. More for exceptional effort or difficulty.

**44. Do final walkthrough systematically:** Every cabinet, closet, corner. Check garage, attic, storage. People always forget something.

## Post-Move Tips

**45. Unpack strategically:** Bedroom and bathroom first for function. Kitchen next for meals. Rest can wait.

**46. Break down boxes immediately:** Flattened boxes take minimal space. Keeping house clear maintains sanity during unpacking.

**47. Update address everywhere:** DMV within 10 days, voter registration within 30. Set calendar reminders.

**48. Meet neighbors early:** They're future package receivers, emergency contacts, local knowledge sources. Introduce yourself.

**49. Document any damage immediately:** Photos, written notes, filed claims. Waiting weakens your position.

**50. Leave honest reviews:** Help others find good movers or avoid bad ones. Your experience matters to future movers.

## Bonus LA Tips

**The traffic factor:** Everything takes longer in LA. Build in buffers. Rush hour starts at 3 PM.

**The parking reality:** There's never enough. Ever. Plan accordingly. Have backup plans.

**The weather surprise:** "Perfect weather" is marketing. Heat waves, rain, and wind happen. Prepare for anything.

**The distance deception:** "Only 10 miles" means nothing in LA. Time and terrain matter more.
