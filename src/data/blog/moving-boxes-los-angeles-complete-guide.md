## Box Sizes and Their Uses

**Small boxes (1.5 cubic feet, 16"×12"×12"):**Perfect for books (never larger!). Heavy items like tools. Canned goods and pantry items. Electronics and cables. Small appliances. Dense items preventing overweight. SOS Moving price: $4 each.

**Medium boxes (3.0 cubic feet, 18"×18"×16"):**Most versatile size needed most. Kitchen items and dishes. Toys and games. Office supplies. Clothes and shoes. Everything else basically. SOS Moving price: $5 each.

**Large boxes (4.5 cubic feet, 18"×18"×24"):**Lightweight bulky items only. Pillows and comforters. Lampshades (nothing heavy!). Stuffed animals. Coats and jackets. Artificial plants. SOS Moving price: $6 each.

**Specialty boxes worth buying:**

**Wardrobe boxes (24"×24"×40"):**Hanging clothes stay wrinkle-free. $12-20 each to buy. SOS Moving provides free (must return). Holds 2 feet of closet. Worth it for good clothes.

**Dish pack boxes (18"×18"×28"):**Double-walled for protection. Cell dividers included sometimes. $7-12 each typically. Essential for china/glassware. Prevents most breakage.

**Mirror/picture boxes (adjustable):**Telescope for custom fit. Protect flat items. $8-15 each usually. TVs fit sometimes. Artwork stays safe.

## Box Alternatives

**Suitcases you own:**Perfect for heavy items. Wheels and handles included. Protected interiors. Stack well. Already paid for.

**Plastic bins:**Reusable after move. Stackable and strong. See-through helpful. Waterproof protection. More expensive initially.

**Laundry baskets:**Odd-shaped items fit. Easy carrying built-in. No packing/unpacking. Immediately useful.

**Garbage bags:**Last resort only. Clothes and linens. Easily torn/punctured. Hard to stack. Confusing to label.

**Original packaging:**Electronics and appliances. Perfect protection guaranteed. Maintains warranty sometimes. Resale value preserved.

## Smart Box Strategy

**Buy new for:**Kitchen dishes/glassware. Electronics and valuables. Heavy items (books). Bottom stack boxes. Important documents.

**Use free for:**Garage items. Linens and towels. Toys and games. Non-fragile everything. Donation items.

**Specialty splurges:**Wardrobe for interview suits. Dish packs for china. Picture boxes for art. TV boxes for large screens.

## Box Preparation Tips

**Reinforcement essentials:**Tape bottom seam twice. Cross-pattern for heavy. Reinforce corners. Double-box fragile. Never exceed 50 pounds.

**Labeling system:**Room name large/clear. Contents list helpful. "Fragile" when applicable. "This side up" arrows. Number boxes sequentially.

**Color coding:**Assign room colors. Use colored markers/tape. Speeds unloading significantly. Everyone understands quickly.
