Congratulations, you’ve made it! The truck is gone, the boxes are in, and you’re standing in your new home. After the whirlwind of packing and planning, it’s common to feel a bit lost and ask, "What now?" Don’t worry. The key to a smooth transition is to tackle the first few hours and days with a clear plan. This checklist from SOS Moving will guide you through the essential first steps to turning your new house into a home.

#### **The First Few Hours: Securing the Essentials**

Before you start unpacking, focus on these critical tasks to ensure your new environment is safe and functional.

- **Do a Walkthrough:** Before the movers leave, walk through every room to check for any damage to your property that may have occurred during the move.- **Locate the Essentials:** Find your circuit breaker box and main water shutoff valve. You’ll want to know where these are *before* you have an emergency.- **Start Basic Cleaning:** You don’t need to deep clean the whole house, but wipe down kitchen counters and clean one bathroom. This will make the first 24 hours much more comfortable.- **Unpack the "Essentials Box":** This is the box you packed with everything you’d need immediately. Find it and keep it accessible. It should have toilet paper, snacks, chargers, medications, and basic toiletries.

#### **The First 24 Hours: Getting Comfortable**

The goal for day one is to set up a functional living space so you can rest and recharge.

- **Assemble the Beds:** A good night's sleep is non-negotiable. Make setting up your beds the top priority. You’ll be grateful for it after a long day.- **Set Up the Bathroom:** Unpack towels, a shower curtain, soap, and your toiletries. A hot shower will feel like a luxury.- **Connect Major Appliances:** Plug in your refrigerator so it can start cooling, and connect your stove and microwave if necessary.- **Plan Your First Meal:** Don't try to cook a gourmet dinner. Plan for something simple, like ordering a pizza. Make sure you have disposable plates and cutlery in your essentials box.

#### **The First Week: Settling In**

Now that the immediate needs are met, you can start the process of truly settling in.

- **Unpack Systematically:** Don't try to do everything at once. Unpack room by room, starting with the kitchen, then bedrooms, then living areas.- **Set Up Utilities & Change Your Address:** Even if you scheduled services to start, double-check that your internet, gas, and electricity are all working correctly. Start the official change of address process with the post office and update your information for banks, subscriptions, etc.- **Test Smoke Detectors:** For your safety, check that all smoke and carbon monoxide detectors have fresh batteries and are working properly.- **Explore Your New Neighborhood:** Take a walk or drive around. Locate the nearest grocery store, pharmacy, and park. Getting familiar with your surroundings will make you feel at home faster.

Moving into a new house is an exciting new chapter. By following a structured checklist, you can manage the initial chaos and focus on the joy of creating your new space. Welcome home!
