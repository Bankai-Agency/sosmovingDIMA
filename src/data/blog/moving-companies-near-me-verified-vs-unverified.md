That "moving companies near me" search returns 200+ results, but only 40 are actual licensed movers. The rest? Guys with trucks, broker middlemen, and outright scams. ## What "Verified" Actually Means

**Verified moving companies have:**

- CPUC license (California Public Utilities Commission)- USDOT number (for interstate)- General liability insurance- Workers compensation- Physical business address- Business license- Clean background checks- Actual employees

**Verification takes 10 minutes. Skipping it costs thousands.**

## The Unverified Reality

**What unverified "movers" are:**

- Random guys from Craigslist- Unlicensed contractors- Day laborers with rental trucks- Broker fronts- Identity thieves- Actual criminals

**Their setup:**

- Gmail email address- Google Voice number- No physical address- Cash only- No contracts- Fake names

They disappear with your stuff.

## How to Verify in 5 Steps

**Step 1: Ask for CPUC number**Every California mover needs one. No exceptions.

**Step 2: Verify at CPUC website**cpuc.ca.gov/Enforcement/Licensing/Household-Goods-CarriersEnter company name or number.

**Step 3: Check insurance**Must show "Active" with current dates.

**Step 4: Google the address**Street View should show actual business, not apartment.

**Step 5: Call the number**Professional answer? Or personal cell?

**All verified? Proceed. Any red flags? Run.**

## Broker vs Carrier Confusion

**Brokers (avoid):**

- Don't own trucks- Sell your move- Take commission- Zero accountability- You don't know actual mover

**Carriers (actual movers):**

- Own trucks- Employ movers- Direct responsibility- Insurance coverage- Who you want

Always ask: "Are you the actual carrier?"
