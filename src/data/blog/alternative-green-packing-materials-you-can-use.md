### Use what you have at home – Blankets, sheets, towels

The first rule of “going green” is to use what you already have. By purchasing new materials, you are adding to their demand, which invariably leads to their further production. If everyone was to use the items they already have available, there would be less need for new materials and the production would slow down. Therefore, you can use your towels, blankets, and sheets as your packing supplies, as opposed to purchasing specialized ones. **They will do the job almost as effectively as their counterparts**, anyway. All you need is a bit of know-how. You can ask your [Los Angeles interstate movers](https://sosmovingla.net/services/interstate-move/) for advice on that matter. They will be happy to assist you and make your move even greener! It is a win-win situation so do not hesitate to ask for advice.

### Newspaper

If you have old newspaper lying around, you can utilize them in your packing efforts to great effect. They are **particularly valuable in protecting glassware, as well as filling empty space in your boxes.** They will serve as a great replacement for packing paper, for example. However, you need to be a bit careful, as they often contain traces of minerals. These are not harmful to you, though, but you still might want to exercise caution. Especially when packing anything that will go near your mouth. That being said, it is perfectly safe to do so if you take some time to clean the items after transportation. If you don’t have any newspaper at hand, you can acquire them dirt cheap from your local recycling office.

### Biodegradable bubble wrap and packing peanuts

Bubble wrap and packing peanuts are a staple of relocations around the world. These materials are so useful, it is hard to imagine relocating a household or an office without them. However, they are made out of plastic and are not really all that reusable. This means that they leave something to be desired when it comes to green moves. But did you know that there are green, biodegradable, options available nowadays? They **might be a bit more difficult to track down** but are there for you to use. By using these supplies, your relocation’s “green level” is going to soar! You can *ask your movers or your local stores about where to purchase these supplies, or you can easily order them from the internet*. Either way, they are a great addition to your moving effort.

### Another alternative green packing materials – Reusable plastic bins

You might consider plastic to be everything but green. And in most cases, you would be right. However, **reusable plastic bins are great containers for your items due to their small weight**. By using them, you will be [creating fewer emissions](https://www.fiaregion1.com/lightcarlowcarbon/), simple as that. Furthermore, they are almost infinitely reusable. This means that there is no need for large quantities of these items to be produced, as they can be used over and over. Lastly, they can be recycled at the end of their lifespan, which is quite considerable.

### Corrugated paper roll

A replacement to standard paper rolls, the **corrugated roll is actually made from recycled paper**. It is the item of choice for many conscious moving companies, as well as shipping centers worldwide. Sustainability is the key here, as a corrugated paper roll can be reused multiple times. And once it reaches the end of its shelf life, it can be perfectly recycled!

### Upcycled materials

Lastly, you can always look for “upcycled” packing supplies. This form of eco-friendly packaging involves **utilizing previously-used packing supplies**. You can find these with some local moving companies, or on sale on sites such as Craigslist. Oftentimes, you will be able to acquire them for free! They are a great way to increase the sustainability of relocations everywhere and are one of the best alternative green packing materials you can use.
