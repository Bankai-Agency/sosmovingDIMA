## Moving with a baby requires amazing organizational skills

You will have to be prepared to really step up to deal with this challenge.* The thing is, moving with your baby is really hard. You have to be prepared to adjust to all kinds of different situations.* So, here is where you start- Your baby is probably used to having meals at particular times of the day,** and that is the only time where you are absolutely necessary.** Bathing, changing, and putting down to sleep is something others can take care of as well.
![a baby](../../assets/cdn/645ab1d97922878b6f5bef7f/645ab1d97922875e1c5bf46b_jonathan-borba-CgWTqYxHEkg-unsplash-scaled-e1602904778699.jpeg)*You will surely do a great job if you organize and your baby will be as happy as ever*
So, start from there. Make a timeline of your baby’s meals and work around them. Some moms choose to use the best pumps and avoid having to nurse at certain times.* Or, some babies don’t have their schedule made up yet.* Some moms have to feed their babies formulas, and they will have more time to deal with the move. No matter what group you belong to, your [residential movers](https://sosmovingla.net/services/residential-move/) **need to know when is the moving day**. This way, you will be able to know what your tasks are as well. No matter what your feeding schedule is, you will just have to work around it.

## Declutter before you start packing your baby’s room

You might have this under control already, and there will be nothing to declutter in the first place. *But, that is not very likely*. **Go through your baby’s old clothes**. They grow up so fast that you probably have a bunch of things in their closets, that they are too big for. You can give these clothes away, or [donate them](https://www.goodneighbors.us/shelter?gclid=CjwKCAjwiaX8BRBZEiwAQQxGx81zICGjljJezmM9eTRattXcPpMKUoMUDcUtDc6Eija0yzayd-Q0QBoCVNMQAvD_BwE), it’s your choice.

If you want to keep the baby clothes for your next child, you can do that as well. The best option would be to get a storage unit and keep them there. Once your moving day gets close, your [long-distance movers](https://sosmovingla.net/services/long-distance-movers/) **will be able to move them to your new home.** Or, another storage unit*. Other than the baby clothes, make sure you get rid of everything that you feel like you don’t really need.* Your move will be much easier this way.

## You should make sure you know when to pack your baby’s room

Packing is definitely the part you have to focus on since it takes the most of your time. That is why you have to plan it and do everything according to that plan. *First of all, you should know that choosing to pack your baby’s room is not something you just do.* You should make sure you declutter first. But, once you are done with that part, you can be sure that** the packing will be the next step**. But, this is not something you want to hurry up with. Baby’s bedroom should be the last room you pack and the first room you unpack once you get to your new home. This is why you should leave everything unpacked just yet, or at least, most of the things.

As you already know, the things that you don’t need, the ones that are way too large or too small for your child, should be packed first*. You can store them, or keep them in the room, the choice is yours.* Now that you packed those things, keep using the **ones that you need until everything else is packed**. Once your home is ready to be moved, you should simply move and unpack the baby room first. That is the only way for you to be able to take care of your child the entire way.
