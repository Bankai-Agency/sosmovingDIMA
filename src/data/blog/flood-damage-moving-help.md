The water recedes, leaving destroyed belongings, dangerous mold potential, and 72 hours to save what's salvageable. Los Angeles may average only 15 inches of rain annually, but when atmospheric rivers hit or pipes burst, flood damage devastates homes instantly. After helping hundreds through water emergencies, here's how professional flood damage moving services work, what's salvageable, and why the first 48 hours determine everything.

## LA Flood Reality

**Common flood causes:**

- Atmospheric rivers (increasing)- Flash floods in canyons- Burst pipes (most common)- Sewage backups- Neighbor's water heater- Roof failures- Foundation issues

**High-risk areas:**

- Hollywood Hills (mudslides)- Beach cities (high tides)- Valley (poor drainage)- Downtown (old infrastructure)

**One inch of water = $25,000 damage average**

## The 48-Hour Window

**Why speed matters:**

**0-24 hours:**

- Water extraction possible- Items salvageable- Mold prevention possible

**24-48 hours:**

- Mold begins growing- Materials deteriorating- Salvage declining

**48-72 hours:**

- Mold established- Secondary damage- Health hazards

**After 72 hours:**Most items unsalvageable

**Every hour counts exponentially**

## Emergency Response Protocol

**Immediate moving needs:**

- Safety assessment first- Power/gas secured- Document everything- Remove salvageable items- Begin drying process- Coordinate restoration

**Professional flood movers provide:**

- Emergency response (2-4 hours)- Water-safe equipment- Protective gear- Documentation assistance- Coordination with restoration

## What's Salvageable

**Usually can save:**

- Solid wood furniture (dried properly)- Metal items- Glass/ceramics- Some electronics (if acted fast)- Plastic items- Important documents (freeze-dried)

**Usually destroyed:**

- Particle board furniture- Mattresses- Upholstered furniture- Carpets/rugs- Drywall- Insulation- Books (unless special treatment)

**Gray water/sewage:** Nothing salvageable

## Documentation Requirements

**For insurance claims:**

**Before moving anything:**

- Video walk-through- Photo every room- Close-ups of damage- Serial numbers visible- Water line heights- Time stamps essential

**During removal:**

- Inventory everything- Categorize: Save/Toss/Maybe- Photo damaged items- Keep samples sometimes- Receipt for services

**Critical for claims success**

## Health and Safety

**Flood water dangers:**

- Bacteria/pathogens- Chemical contamination- Sewage possible- Mold spores- Structural hazards- Electrical risks

**Required protection:**

- N95 masks minimum- Waterproof boots- Gloves- Eye protection- Tetanus current

**Never enter without assessment**
