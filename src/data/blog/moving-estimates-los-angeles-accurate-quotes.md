## Types of Moving Estimates

**Non-binding estimates (most common):**Approximate cost based on estimated weight/time. Final price can increase or decrease. Typically accurate within 10-20%. Requires honest inventory disclosure. SOS Moving uses for local moves.

**Binding estimates:**Fixed price regardless of actual weight/time. Cannot exceed quoted amount. Often padded 20-30% for safety. Good for budgeting precisely. Common for long-distance moves.

**Not-to-exceed estimates:**Price can decrease but not increase. Best for consumers theoretically. Movers rarely offer anymore. Usually includes many exclusions. Read fine print carefully.

## Factors Affecting Your Estimate

**Property specifics:**Floor level adds time significantly. Elevator access saves time. Long carries from parking. Stairs multiply effort required. Narrow passages slow progress.

**Volume and weight:**More stuff = more time. Heavy items need extra movers. Packed vs unpacked differs greatly. Clutter adds 20-30% time. Organization saves money significantly.

**Distance considerations:**Under 10 miles standard rates. Over 10 miles triggers Double Drive Time (CPUC regulation). Traffic timing affects duration. Multiple locations add time.

**Special circumstances:**

- Pianos: Add $200 flat fee- Items over 300 lbs: Add $150- Additional truck needed: Add $200- COI required (high-rises): Add $50- Storage overnight: Add $200

## Getting Accurate Estimates

**Provide complete information:**List every room honestly. Mention heavy/oversized items. Disclose access challenges. Include garage/attic/storage. Admit if disorganized.

**Visual estimates best:**In-person most accurate always. Video calls second best. Photos help significantly. Phone-only least reliable. Item lists often wrong.

**Questions to ask:**"Is this binding or non-binding?" "What's explicitly included?" "What could increase costs?" "What's your not-to-exceed policy?" "How do you handle overtime?"

**Red flags in estimates:**

- Extremely low prices ($50-75/hour)- No minimum hours mentioned- Vague about what's included- Cubic feet measurements (confusing intentionally)- Large deposits required (25%+ suspicious)- Cash only payment- No written documentation

## Comparing Estimates Properly

**Apples to apples:**Compare hourly vs hourly. Flat rate vs flat rate. Include all fees. Factor in minimums. Consider included services.

**Hidden costs to uncover:**

- Travel time charges- Fuel surcharges- Stair/elevator fees- Packing material costs- Insurance charges- Credit card fees- Tip expectations

**True cost calculation:**Base estimate + potential additions + insurance + tips = real cost. SOS Moving includes everything except tips in our quotes.

## Avoiding Estimate Scams

**The lowball trap:**Quote $500, charge $2,000 on truck. "Cubic footage increased" excuse. "Stairs weren't mentioned" addition. Belongings held hostage.

**The padding scheme:**Overestimate intentionally. Fear drives acceptance. Pocket the difference. Common with binding estimates.

**Verification steps:**Check CPUC license. Verify insurance certificates. Read reviews extensively. Get multiple estimates. Trust instincts.
