Typing "movers near me" into Google returns hundreds of Los Angeles moving companies, from legitimate professionals to guys with a truck listed on Craigslist. The difference between choosing right and wrong can mean damaged furniture, surprise fees, or worse—held hostage belongings. After years in the LA moving industry, here's exactly how to separate professional movers from potential disasters.

## Red Flags to Run From

**No physical address:** Only PO Box or residential address? They'll disappear with your deposit.

**Too-good-to-be-true quotes:** $50/hour for 4 movers? That's below minimum wage. Expect surprise charges.

**Cash only:** Legitimate companies like SOS Moving accept cards. Cash-only means no accountability.

**No license number:** California requires CPUC licensing. No number = illegal operation.

**Borrowed photos:** Reverse image search their trucks. Stock photos = fake company.

**New website:** Check domain age. Scammers create sites, scam, disappear, repeat.

**Aggressive sales:** "Book today or price doubles!" Professional movers don't use high-pressure tactics.

## Essential Questions to Ask

**"What's your CPUC license number?"**Every legal California mover has one. SOS Moving and legitimate companies provide it immediately.

**"Can I visit your office?"**Real companies have real locations. Excuses mean they're operating from someone's garage.

**"What's included in your hourly rate?"**SOS Moving includes truck, fuel, equipment, insurance. Others charge separately for everything.

**"Do you charge double drive time?"**California law requires it for 10+ miles. If they say no, they're lying or will surprise you later.

**"What's your damage policy?"**Should be clear, in writing. "We're careful" isn't a policy.

**"Can you provide recent references?"**Last month's customers, not reviews from 2019.

## Verify Before You Trust

**Check CPUC license:**Go to CPUC website. Enter company name. Verify active status, insurance, complaints.

**Google the phone number:**Same number associated with multiple company names? Scam operation using different identities.

**Read recent reviews:**Look for patterns. All 5-stars posted same week? Fake. Mix of 4-5 stars over time? Real.

**Check Better Business Bureau:**Not foolproof but shows complaint patterns.

**Ask for insurance certificate:**Legitimate movers email it immediately. Excuses = red flag.

**Crew arrives and:**

- Different company name on truck- Demands cash before starting- "Forgot" equipment, needs more money- Says quote was "per mover per hour"- Wants to renegotiate price- No uniforms or identification

Professional movers arrive prepared, uniformed, and honor original quotes.

## Online Review Investigation

**Real reviews include:**

- Specific details- Both positives and negatives- Response from company- Reviewer has other reviews- Photos of actual move

**Fake reviews obvious by:**

- Generic praise- No specifics- All posted within days- Reviewers have no other activity- Similar writing style
