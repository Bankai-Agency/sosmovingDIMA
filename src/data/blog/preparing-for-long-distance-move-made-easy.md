## Schedule when preparing for a long distance move

You individually might not be a person that is doing everything according to a schedule, but a long-distance move doesn’t leave much space for improvisation. *A good move takes weeks or even months, so try and create a plan of action.* Our [movers California to Florida](https://sosmovingla.net/services/interstate-move/california-to-florida/) know the best how messy it can be when people don’t plan well so they need to take over. Create goals that you need to complete in certain time periods. It will motivate you more, but also give you more purpose and intent to continue preparing for the big move. Making a list is also a great way to keep yourself in check. As the moving day approaches, you’ll know what needs to be done, and **the schedule will be a great tool to help you out.**

## Organize

While a move can make you think about more than just one thing at the same time, *it’s very important to stay calm and organized.* For example, when packing you’ll need to take care of things like important documents, birth certificates, jewelry, and other items that are extremely valuable and need special attention. You don’t want them to get lost in the disorder that can happen during a long-distance move. This is also a great time to get to know policies about the city or state you’re moving to. Things like registering to vote or getting a driver’s license might be completely different from where you’re from to where you’re moving.

## Pack

As this is a long-distance move it won’t be a good idea if you take moving boxes from your local grocery stores or online. Your items need to be packed and wrapped the best they can. They have a long way ahead of them so protection is a must. For that reason,** it’s not a bad idea to invest in **[**packing services**](https://sosmovingla.net/services/packing-services/)**, but also in packing materials like moving boxes and bubble wrap for starters.** Another important task when it comes to packing is labeling.* Every box needs its label, so make sure it’s visible.* Also setting aside boxes for essentials and important documents won’t be a mistake either.

## Save money preparing for a long distance move

When people are moving any type of move it can get expensive, and a long-distance move can’t come without certain costs. For that reason, it’s not a bad idea to save money if there is space to do so. One of the best tips for that is to *move off-season*. The movers will have less work and they will be cheaper and more available. Also moving during the mid-week period will be cheaper than the one during the weekend when everybody is moving. If you implement these tips you’ll be able to every dollar count.

## Declutter

Decluttering your space is the perfect thing to do before a move. Especially if you’re moving out of the city or even the state. It’s finally time to sit down and write down all the items you really need. Get out a piece of paper and a pen and divide items into these three groups:

- **Sell – **These are the items that you might find some use, but you really just don’t need them. But if you don’t have use for them, that doesn’t mean somebody won’t find value in them. So do a garage sale or sell them online.- **Donate – **Donating to the people in need is a very unselfish thing to do, but make sure you are ready to part ways with those items. Make sure everything you give out is in good condition. There are many places where you can [donate clothes](https://dressforsuccess.org/), furniture, or even food, so find the one that suits you the best and call them up.- **Keep – **Items that fall in this category are first to get in the boxes. If you need help with important items for you, try asking yourself if your life would change without those items. If your answer is positive then that item needs to be in this section.

## Preparing for a long distance move full of energy

If your moving day is near, you should be relaxing and checking your plan. A long-distance move should be planned well, so** a couple of days before the move itself should be days for controlling everything and relaxing.** You’ve already done a lot, so it’s a good idea to have a nice long sleep the night of the move. Obviously, the trip itself is also demanding so make sure you’re full of energy and hydrated during the ride.

You can’t always make a move go smoothly and without problems, but **if you start preparing for a long-distance move in due time it will be much easier**.
