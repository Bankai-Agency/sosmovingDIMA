The foreclosure notice arrives, and suddenly you have 30-60 days to leave a home you've lived in for years. The shame, stress, and overwhelming logistics make people freeze when action is needed most. After helping hundreds through foreclosure moves in Los Angeles, we know the resources available, the timeline reality, and how to protect what matters when everything feels lost.

## Understanding the Timeline

**Foreclosure isn't immediate:**

- Notice of Default: 90 days to cure- Notice of Sale: 21 days minimum- Eviction after sale: 30-60 days- Total process: 4-8 months typically

**You have more time than you think.**

**Critical dates:**

- Auction date (home still yours until sold)- New owner notification- Cash for keys deadline- Eviction filing- Sheriff lockout date

Each stage has different rights and options.

## Cash for Keys Programs

**What it is:**Bank pays you to leave voluntarily, avoiding eviction costs.

**Typical offers:**

- $3,000-10,000 common- Sometimes up to $20,000- Negotiable often

**Requirements:**

- Leave by specific date- Property in good condition- No damage/stripping- Keys turned over

**This money funds your move and fresh start.**

## Free Moving Resources

**HUD-approved counseling:**

- Free consultation- Resource connections- Moving assistance programs- Call: 1-800-569-4287

**Neighborhood assistance:**

- Churches often help- Community organizations- Mutual aid groups- No judgment, just help

**Government programs:**

- Emergency assistance (DPSS)- CalAIM housing support- Rapid rehousing programs- Call 211 for connections

## Legal Rights During Foreclosure

**You still have rights:**

- Cannot be locked out without court order- Utilities cannot be shut off- Personal property is yours- Harassment is illegal- Time to retrieve belongings

**Tenant rights (if renting foreclosed property):**

- 90-day notice required- Lease may be honored- Relocation assistance possible- Cash for keys negotiable

**Document everything. Know your rights.**

## Storage Solutions

**If you can't move everything immediately:**

**Free/cheap storage options:**

- Friends/family garages- Church storage programs- Community storage shares- Negotiated storage with new owner

**Professional storage:**

- First month specials common- No credit check options- Month-to-month terms- Climate control for valuables

**Storage buying time while you figure out next steps.**

## What to Save First

**Priority items:**

- All documents (birth certificates, SSN, tax records)- Medications- Family photos/videos- Computers/hard drives- Jewelry/valuables- Children's special items- Tools for work- Professional clothes

**Second priority:**

- Furniture you love- Kitchen essentials- Bedding- Seasonal clothes- Electronics

**Let go:**

- Old furniture- Broken items- Unused belongings- Replaceable goods

## Housing Options

**Immediate/temporary:**

- Extended stay hotels- Room rentals- Staying with family- Shelter systems (last resort)

**Longer term:**

- Apartment with co-signer- Rent-by-week places- Mobile home parks- Shared housing

**Credit issues:**

- Look for private landlords- Offer larger deposit- Get recommendation letters- Explain situation honestly

## Avoiding Scams

**Foreclosure attracts predators:**

**Red flags:**

- Upfront fees for "rescue"- Deed transfer requests- Guaranteed loan modifications- Pressure tactics- Too-good-to-be-true offers

**Legitimate help is usually free.**

**Verify everything:**

- HUD-approved only- Licensed attorneys- Established nonprofits- Government programs

## Moving Day Strategy

**If eviction scheduled:**

- Be packed before date- Remove everything valuable- Take photos of condition- Turn in keys properly- Avoid sheriff involvement
