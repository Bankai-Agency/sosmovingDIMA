## The Hidden Costs of Self-Storage

**What they advertise:**"First month only $89!"

**What you actually pay:**

- Monthly rent after promo: $250-450- Mandatory insurance: $25/month- Admin fees: $30- Lock: $20- Climate control: +30%- Truck rental (2x): $300- Gas: $100- Moving supplies: $150- Your time: 20+ hours

**Real monthly cost: $400-600 plus labor**

## Breaking Down Storage Unit Expenses

**Public Storage in LA example (10×10 unit):**Month 1: $89 (promo)Month 2-12: $329/monthInsurance: $25/monthAdmin fee: $25Lock: $15-30Total first year: $3,994

Plus you haven't moved anything yet. Add U-Haul rental ($29.95 + $1.29/mile) for move-in and move-out, and you're looking at $400+ in transportation alone.

## The Physical Cost Nobody Mentions

Loading a storage unit means:

- Lifting everything twice (in and out)- Playing Tetris to maximize space- Climbing over items to reach back boxes- Risking injury on heavy furniture- Multiple exhausting trips

The average storage unit user makes 8 trips during their rental period. That's 8 truck rentals, 8 tanks of gas, 8 days of physical labor.

## Common Storage Unit Problems

**Security issues:** Even "secure" facilities report break-ins. Ground-floor units especially vulnerable.

**Climate problems:** Non-climate controlled units in LA reach 100°+ in summer. Electronics fail, wood warps, photos stick together.

**Access hassles:** "24/7 access" requires driving to facility, remembering gate codes, navigating dark hallways.

**Hidden damage:** Items shifted, boxes crushed, moisture damage discovered months later.
