## The best strategies for packing musical instruments

Your only goal when packing instruments is to *ensure that they arrive at their destination in one piece*. Here’s how to do it:

- **Use the original case**. If you have the original case, your job is already done. These cases are specifically designed to protect delicate instruments when carried around. You can fill out any leftover gaps with old clothes or packing paper – just in case.- **Cardboard boxes**. If you don’t have the original case you can use ordinary boxes. First, apply several layers of protective materials around the instrument. Then, place the instrument inside the box. Use packing paper to fill out any leftover gaps to prevent the instrument from moving around the box.- [Label the box as fragile](https://www.wikihow.com/Pack-Your-Fragile-Items#:~:text=Use%20paper%20or%20bubble%20wrap,larger%2C%20soft%20articles%20of%20clothing.). Use marker pens to write fragile or instruments on the box to make sure that your movers handle those boxes with extra care.
