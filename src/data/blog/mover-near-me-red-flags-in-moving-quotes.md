That "mover near me" search returned dozens of quotes, ranging from suspiciously cheap to mysteriously vague. The difference between legitimate quotes and scam setups isn't always obvious until your belongings are held hostage on a truck. After reviewing thousands of moving quotes and their outcomes, here are the red flags that scream "run" versus signs of legitimate movers.

## Immediate Red Flags

**"Per cubic foot" pricing for local moves:**Local moves are hourly. Period. Cubic foot = broker or scam.

**No physical address:**Only PO Box or residential address? They'll vanish with your deposit.

**Quote without questions:**Never asked about stairs? Building type? Special items? They're guessing.

**Massive deposit required:**Legitimate movers: $50-200 depositScammers: 25-50% upfront

**Cash only payment:**No paper trail = no accountability. Real companies take cards.

## Suspiciously Low Quotes

**The $50/hour special:**

- Minimum wage in LA: $17.28- Two movers at minimum: $35/hour- Add truck, gas, insurance: Impossible

**What happens next:**"Oh, that's per mover per hour""Plus truck rental separately""Insurance is mandatory extra""Fuel surcharge applies"

Your $50/hour becomes $200/hour on moving day.

**SOS Moving transparent pricing:**2 movers: $119-145/hour all includedNo surprise multipliers

## Vague Quote Language

**Red flag phrases:**

- "Estimated between $X and $Y" (huge range)- "Approximately" (everywhere)- "Subject to change"- "Based on visual estimate"- "Final price determined on truck"- "Additional fees may apply"

**Legitimate quote language:**

- "Hourly rate: $159/hour for 3 movers"- "Minimum: 3 hours"- "Includes: truck, fuel, equipment"- "Additional charges only for: piano ($200)"

Specific = legitimate. Vague = danger.

## Missing Information

**Every quote must include:**

- Company name and license- Number of movers- Hourly rate or flat rate- Minimum hours- What's included- What's extra- Insurance coverage- Payment methods

**Missing any of these? Red flag.**

**Scam quotes often missing:**

- CPUC license number- Insurance details- Physical address- Cancellation policy- Damage procedures

## The "Binding Estimate" Trap

**Sounds good:**"Binding estimate - price won't change!"

**Reality:**Binding only if inventory 100% accurate. Miss one box? Contract void. Price triples.

**How they manipulate:**

- Rush inventory list- Don't document everything- "Find" extra items on moving day- Claim you added stuff- Demand new payment

**Legitimate binding estimates:**Detailed inventory with photos. Clear conditions. Reasonable overage policy.

## Insurance Red Flags

**Scam insurance language:**

- "Full coverage included!" (Never true)- "Everything protected!" (Legal impossibility)- "$100,000 coverage!" (But $1,000 deductible)- "Basic coverage" (Worth nothing)

**Real insurance language:**

- "$0.60 per pound included"- "Full value protection available"- "Deductible: $250"- "Exclusions: cash, jewelry"

If they can't explain coverage clearly, they're hiding something.

## Time Pressure Tactics

**Red flag urgency:**

- "Price only good today!"- "Book now or rate doubles!"- "Only one crew available!"- "Special ending in 1 hour!"

**Reality:**Legitimate movers have consistent pricing. Urgency = scam tactic.

**SOS Moving approach:**Same rates all week. Book when ready. No fake deadlines.

## The Subcontracting Surprise

**Warning signs:**

- "We work with partner companies"- "Assigned crew determined later"- "Local teams handle delivery"- Different company name on quote

**What happens:**You hired Company A. Company B shows up. Company C delivers. Nobody responsible for damage.

**Legitimate companies:**"Our employees, our trucks, our insurance."

## Communication Red Flags

**Scam communication:**

- Only text messages- Gmail/Yahoo email addresses- No company email domain- Won't provide written quotes- Pushy sales calls- Unprofessional language

**Professional communication:**

- Company email addresses- Written documentation- Phone and email available- Patient explanations- No pressure

## The "Visual Estimate" Scam

**How it works:**"Based on your description: $500"

**Moving day:**"Oh, you have a couch? That's extra""Boxes are heavier than expected""Didn't know about stairs"

Price becomes $1,500.

**Legitimate estimates:**Either proper in-person assessment or detailed questionnaire covering everything.

## Quote Comparison Red Flags

**When quotes are wildly different:**

Company A: $400Company B: $1,200Company C: $450Company D: $1,100

Companies A and C are lying. Real costs don't vary 300%.

**Normal variation:**20-30% difference maximum for same service.

## Contract Red Flags

**Never sign contracts with:**

- Blank spaces- "To be determined" items- No cancellation policy- No damage procedures- Arbitration only clauses- No company signature

**Always require:**

- Complete pricing- Both signatures- Clear terms- Damage process- Payment terms- Date/time specific

## Payment Red Flags

**Scam payment demands:**

- Large cash deposit- Wire transfers- Venmo/Zelle to personal accounts- Payment before service- No receipts offered

**Legitimate payment:**

- Small deposit (credit card)- Payment upon completion- Multiple payment options- Professional receipts- Company name on charges

## Questions That Expose Scams

Ask these. Scammers stumble:

- "What's your CPUC license number?"- "Can I visit your office?"- "Who exactly are your employees?"- "What happens if something breaks?"- "Can you explain the insurance?"- "Why is your price so different?"

Legitimate movers answer immediately. Scammers get defensive.

## Trust Your Gut

**If something feels wrong:**

- Too good to be true- Pushy sales tactics- Evasive answers- Unprofessional behavior- Complicated explanations- Multiple red flags

It's probably wrong. Keep searching.
