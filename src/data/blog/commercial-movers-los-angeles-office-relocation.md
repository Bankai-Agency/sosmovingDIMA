## Commercial vs Residential Moving

**Critical differences:**Timeline precision essential—businesses can't be homeless. IT infrastructure requires specialists. Furniture systems need proper sequencing. Security and confidentiality paramount. Employee coordination complex. Permits and insurance extensive.

**Stakes are higher:**Downtime costs thousands hourly. Lost data catastrophic potentially. Employee productivity affected weeks. Client relationships risk damage. Competition gains advantages. Recovery takes months sometimes.

**Different approach required:**Project management methodology. Detailed planning phases. Multiple site visits. Weekend/night execution. Staged moving possible. Technology focus critical.

## The Commercial Moving Process

**8-12 weeks before:**Initial consultation and assessment. Space planning new location. Budget development. Timeline creation. Vendor coordination begins. Employee communication starts.

**4-8 weeks before:**Detailed inventory creation. Packing supplies ordered. IT infrastructure planning. Permit applications submitted. Building requirements confirmed. Insurance documentation prepared.

**2-4 weeks before:**Packing non-essentials begins. Employee packing training. Label system implemented. Floor plans finalized. Moving weekend selected. Contingency plans developed.

**1 week before:**IT systems backed up. Final packing push. Building notifications sent. Parking reserved. Elevators scheduled. Team assignments confirmed.

**Moving weekend:**Friday evening: IT disconnect. Saturday: Main move. Sunday: Unpacking/setup. Monday morning: Operational.

## IT Infrastructure Moving

**Critical considerations:**Data backup mandatory. Server room climate control. Cable management systems. Static protection essential. Chain of custody documentation. Immediate reinstallation needed.

**Common IT mistakes:**Insufficient labeling causing chaos. No backup creating panic. Improper packing damaging equipment. Lost cables paralyzing systems. Poor coordination extending downtime.

**Best practices:**Color-code everything meticulously. Photograph all connections. Create detailed documentation. Use original packaging ideally. Climate-controlled transport required. IT staff present throughout.
