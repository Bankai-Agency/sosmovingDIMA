## Different types of moving estimates to consider

Take a look at the three most common types of moving estimates you can expect.

- **A non-binding moving estimate **is the most common type of moving quote you will get. Most moving companies provide this quote since it’s the easiest to calculate, and this is how it works. *Once the movers measure the size and weight of your inventory, they will use this information as a deciding factor.* However, non-binding moving estimates are not the best solution for people moving on a tight budget. The initial cost of your move will be just a rough estimation. So, you can expect your moving expenses to pile up at the end.

- **A binding moving estimate **means that the moving company you hire guarantees a fixed price of their services. Although this sounds like the best moving estimate to choose, it’s not that simple. In case your moving costs turn out to be less than the movers anticipated, you will still pay the same amount of money you agreed upon.

- **A binding not-to-exceed estimate **is the most popular quote you will find. When offering moving estimates like this one, movers will provide a rough estimation of your moving costs. This is the most popular quote for interstate and long-distance moves. If you choose a not-to-exceed estimate, you might pay a little bit more for moving services. However, there is a positive side. If your costs get bigger during the moving day, a moving company will guarantee to cover the remaining amount.
