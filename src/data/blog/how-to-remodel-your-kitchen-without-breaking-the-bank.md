## Refreshing instead of replacing

It might seem like a great idea to replace everything. However, all tear-out and replacement projects are more expensive. Since you want to save money, you should probably do something else instead of replacing everything. For example, you can **repaint your kitchen cabinets**. This is a classic way of updating them. Even you can repaint your cabinets and save money by not hiring professionals. Then, you can try refacing them. This is a little bit more expensive than repainting your cabinets but it is cheaper than getting the new ones. After this, you can also update the hardware. Usually, modern knobs and handles will make a huge change to your cabinets. Lastly, you can install new shelves. This is a cheaper option than replacing your cabinets. You can do all this job before [movers California to New York](https://sosmovingla.net/services/interstate-move/california-to-new-york/) deliver your items.

## Do not change your kitchen layout

You should give the same kitchen layout that you had before. Dramatically changing the layout is extremely expensive. The reason is quite simple. If you plan to move the sink, dishwasher, or even the fridge, you will have to hire plumbers. They will have to change plumbing for these appliances and for the sink. This means making holes in your walls for the new pipes, more materials, more manual labor, and so on. As you can see, **this already sounds extremely expensive**. For this reason, you should keep the same layout but you can change certain things. You can try updating your sink, refurbish the dishwasher, or even replacing the fridge. In addition to this, you can also consider adding a kitchen island. This is a great way to get more space and storage without expensive layout changes. It will also look great if you have a kitchen island.

## Remodel your kitchen without breaking the bank by doing some work yourself

If you have time, energy, and you’re good with your hands,* you can try to do some work yourself.* The most common thing most people can do would be interior painting. As mentioned, you can repaint your cabinets, change the color of your walls, and so on. If you are an expert, you can also try tiling, flooring installation, [changing outlets and lights](https://www.dummies.com/home-garden/home-improvement/electrical-wiring/how-to-replace-an-electrical-outlet/), hanging drywall, and installing baseboards. If you do not know how to do all of this, but you still can take some classes. Usually, community colleges would offer how-to classes and small demonstrations for common home projects. However, it is extremely important to remember that you are trying not to spend too much money. If you happen to damage something, it is going to be expensive to replace it.
